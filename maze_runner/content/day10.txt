[[DAY]]
DAY 10 — THE OVERFLOW

[[NARRATIVE]]
Morning arrives and nobody trusts it.

The light comes up the same way it always does, even and artificial and indifferent to what happened in the night, and Aurora's Rest holds its shape under the 90 meter walls the same way it always does, and the waterfall runs the same way it always runs. The camp moves through the first hour like people navigating a room in the dark, careful, slow, hands out, not because they cannot see but because they have stopped trusting what they can see.

Kiwi is alive.

That is the first thing space fan confirms when the light comes up, crouching beside him with two fingers on his pulse and her eyes on his face. His breathing is shallow but consistent. His temperature is down from the fever peak. His eyes open when she speaks his name, track her face slowly, then close again. He does not speak. His hands move occasionally without direction, small involuntary twitches at the fingers, the nervous system still working something out in the language of muscle memory.

He is not turning.

Nobody says this aloud. But it is the operating assumption that every person in Aurora's Rest is running beneath every other thought, the quiet constant calculation of watching someone who was stung by a Quigg and checking whether the math is still holding. space fan changes the dressing on his neck. She keeps her face neutral. She is very good at keeping her face neutral.

Kiwi's eyes open once in the mid-morning and find Rally mally sitting nearby. He looks at him for a long moment with the unfocused clarity of someone still partially inside a fever, then says, very quietly, "It waited for me to see it."

Rally mally says, "I know."

Kiwi closes his eyes again.

Rally mally does not move for a while after that.

Golden is talking.

He has been talking since the camp woke, not loudly, not dramatically, but persistently, the way water finds the low path in stone. He moves between small clusters of people and leaves behind him a residue of questions that sound like observations.

He says to Rabbit, "Every new arrival raises the resource pressure. More mouths. Same walls."

He says to SomeDucks, "The supplies keep coming. You think that is generosity. I think it is preparation."

He says to Cyrus, "Settlement phase is what they tell livestock before the pen gets crowded."

Rabbit listens and says nothing. His face has been getting more complicated since Day 9 and Golden can read faces the way janhk. used to read seams.

Duccarian sits with Frodge near the waterfall edge and watches Golden move through the camp. He does not intervene. He watches the way a person watches weather, tracking the system without expecting to stop it.

SomeDucks sits beside him after a while. "He is not wrong about the pressure."

Duccarian says, "He is not wrong about the facts. He is wrong about what the facts mean."

SomeDucks looks at the waterfall. "What do they mean."

Duccarian watches Frodge shift in his arms. "I do not know yet. But I know the Maze does not prepare for an ending without preparing us first. It wants us functional when it arrives."

Rally mally holds a quiet morning meeting that is less a meeting and more a mutual acknowledgment of reality. He does not pretend the night did not happen. He does not perform confidence. He stands in the center of the camp and speaks plainly.

"Kiwi is alive. He is not turning. space fan is monitoring him. We do not treat him differently." He pauses. "We work today. We build. We eat. We watch the doors."

Golden, from the edge of the circle, says nothing. He simply watches Rally mally's face while he speaks, which is its own kind of commentary.

Lord Kalameet stands near the shelter line through the meeting with his arms at his sides and his attention distributed evenly across the group. He does not perform reassurance but his presence has a stabilizing gravity that Aurora's Rest has not had since before Ut and janhk. died in the second beacon corridor. Trxty sits slightly apart, watching the meeting with the expression of someone reading a document for the second time and checking it against their memory of the first.

Nightmare watches Golden watch Rally mally and says nothing at all. His expression is unreadable in the specific way that means he is reading everything.

The elevator begins at midday.

They hear it before they see it. The sound is different from every previous ascent, longer, heavier, a sustained mechanical strain that carries through the ground in waves rather than the usual rhythmic progression. The vibration reaches the waterfall zone and the basin surface shimmers slightly. The camp feels it in the soles of their feet.

Toad looks up from the fermentation pot. "That is not a normal ascent."

Lysrix is already standing. "No."

The platform takes almost twice as long to reach the surface. When it does, the locking mechanism engages with a shudder that moves through the surrounding stone, and the camp that has gathered at a careful distance looks at what has arrived.

Four people.

Two of them standing. Two of them not.

Polaris and Smart2004 step off the platform with the careful alert stillness of people who were prepared for something but not for this specific something. Polaris takes in the camp in a single sweep, the hut, the cage standing open and empty, Galaxy at the edge of the gathering, and her attention arranges everything she sees into a hierarchy of information without her face showing the process. Smart2004 steps down beside her and immediately looks at the Maze doors, then at the structural lines of the shelter, then at the supply organization, reading the camp like an engineering diagram with people in it.

hollow Camila is carried off the platform by Lord Kalameet, who moves to do it without being asked. She is deeply sedated, completely limp, breathing steadily, her face holding the absence of expression that deep unconsciousness produces. There is something specifically uncomfortable about her stillness that goes beyond the sedation itself, a quality of presence that is too complete, too arranged, like the sleep was placed rather than fallen into.

Mainthemainstar is sedated as well, but differently. There is bruising along the left side of his face and a contusion at the temple that was not caused by the elevator platform. Something happened to him before he arrived. space fan moves to him immediately and her hands find the injury and her expression does the thing it does when she is angry and has decided that anger is a resource she cannot spend right now.

The supply crates come up with them. Four of them. Larger than any previous drop.

Toad opens the first one and goes still for a moment, then starts cataloguing in a voice that is quieter than his normal cataloguing voice, the voice of someone whose hands are moving through something significant.

Advanced medical injectors, more than they have seen in one delivery. Structural materials, metal brackets and joining pins and tensioned cord that Cyrus immediately picks up and turns in his hands with the expression of someone being handed the answer to a question they have been working on for days. Heavy tools, the kind that suggest construction rather than survival. Long-term food stores, dense and portioned for weeks not days. Advanced insulation fabric, the kind that implies the Maze expects them to still be here when the temperature matters.

Cyrus says, slowly, "This is not survival equipment."

Toad says, "No."

"This is settlement equipment."

Toad looks at the crates. "Yes."

Golden appears at the edge of the supply circle. He looks at the crates and then at the four new arrivals and then at the total population of Aurora's Rest and says, "Count the people."

Nobody moves immediately.

Golden says it again, without heat, with the flat insistence of someone stating a logical necessity. "Count them."

Rabbit counts. He does it quietly, moving his eyes around the camp without pointing, including Kiwi lying near the medic hut and hollow Camila being settled inside it and Mainthemainstar beside her and Galaxy in his peripheral vision near the wall.

He says the number.

Nineteen.

The word lands differently than numbers usually land. Not because it is large. Because of what it represents, which is that the Maze has been adding people at a rate that Aurora’s Rest was not meant to sustain this many active variable and has now crossed a threshold that feels structural rather than numerical.

Duccarian says, quietly, from where he is sitting with Frodge, "The Maze is filling the board."

SomeDucks looks at the new arrivals. "For what game."

Nobody answers that.

Polaris has been listening to all of this from near the shelter line. She has not introduced herself beyond her name. She has not asked for orientation. She has been watching Golden specifically, tracking his movement through the group, reading the residue he leaves behind him in conversations. After the counting moment she walks to Rally mally directly.

She says, "The loud one. How long has he been destabilizing your leadership."

Rally mally looks at her. "His name is Golden."

"I know what his name is," Polaris says. "How long."

Rally mally holds her gaze for a moment. "Two days."

Polaris nods once and steps back. She does not elaborate. But she positions herself differently in the camp after that, not beside Rally mally, not visibly aligned, but placed in a way that triangulates with his position across the social geometry of Aurora's Rest.

Smart2004 spends the afternoon near the Maze doors without crossing the threshold, studying the stone seam lines, the door mechanism housing, the corridor air pressure changes. He asks Rabbit questions in the efficient shorthand of someone who has already built most of the answer and needs only specific coordinates to complete it.

Galaxy moves through the afternoon with the controlled unease of an animal whose territory has changed without warning. He circles the perimeter twice. He approaches the new arrivals one by one, not aggressively, just present, reading each of them with the senses his transformation gave him and his human memory gave him access to interpret. When he reaches hollow Camila in the medic hut he stops in the doorway and holds very still for a long moment.

Then he backs away without entering.

Toad watches this from across the camp. He does not ask Galaxy what he smelled.

He does not think he wants to know.

Kiwi reacts to the arrivals in his semi-conscious state in a way that space fan notes and does not explain to anyone yet. When the elevator made its extended ascent, his micro tremors increased in frequency. When the sedated arrivals were carried past him, his breathing changed, shortened, and his hands closed into loose fists that released slowly. He did not wake. But something in him responded to the sedated Peeps specifically, some subconscious register still running on venom-altered frequencies that the conscious mind has not yet regained access to.

space fan adds this to the private record she is keeping in the dirt beside his sleeping spot, which she smooths over each time she adds to it.

The afternoon passes in the particular way long afternoons pass when the weight of a day has been front-loaded and the remaining hours are just the body carrying what the morning already established.

Then Frodge disappears.

Not dramatically. Not with warning. One moment the duck is beside Duccarian near the waterfall edge and the next moment the space is empty and Duccarian's hand is resting on warm ground where Frodge was sitting.

He finds him twenty minutes later near the orchard fence line, waddling back from the direction of the far wall corner with the unhurried certainty of a duck who completed a task and is returning from it. There is a small roll of paper tied to his left leg with a thin strip of cloth.

Duccarian crouches and unties it with hands that are careful and deliberate.

He reads it.

He reads it twice.

Then he holds it against his chest for a moment, looking at the waterfall, and the waterfall runs its smooth indifferent fall into the dark basin below and offers him nothing.

He finds Rally mally near the shelter line and says, very quietly, so that only Rally mally hears, "Schwalbe sent something."

Rally mally looks at him. "What."

Duccarian shows him the note.

Let the carnage begin.
— Schwalbe

Rally mally reads it. His expression does not change in the way that means it has changed completely, just below the surface where leadership does not allow things to show.

He folds the note.

He says, "Who else saw this."

Duccarian says, "Only you."

Rally mally holds the folded note and looks at the camp around him, the nineteen people, the four new arrivals, Kiwi barely conscious near the medic hut, the settlement supplies stacked and sorted by Toad, Galaxy circling the perimeter, Golden watching Rally mally from across the camp with the patient attention of someone who already knows something is wrong and is waiting to see how the leader handles it.

He puts the note in his pocket.

"We say nothing tonight," he says.

Duccarian nods once.

They stand together for a moment looking at Aurora's Rest, the village that the Maze did not plan for, the home built inside a cage, the waterfall falling forever into its engineered dark.

Then they go back to their respective places in the camp and the day continues as though a note does not exist.

Day 10 ends with nineteen people in Aurora's Rest and a note in Rally mally's pocket and a duck asleep against a philosopher's side.

The settlement supplies are stacked and sorted.

The beer in Toad's pot is one day from ready.

Kiwi is breathing.

The Maze is quiet.

And somewhere behind all of it, patient and present and watching every face in Aurora's Rest with the attention of something that has been building toward a specific moment, Schwalbe has announced the next phase with three words and a signature.

The camp does not know this yet.

Rally mally does.

And the weight of that knowledge is exactly the kind of weight that changes how a leader stands when nobody is looking.
