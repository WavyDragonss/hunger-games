[[DAY]]
DAY 11 — THE TOOLS OF WAR

[[NARRATIVE]]
Morning arrives to a camp that is already moving.

Nobody waits for Rally mally to call headcount. Nobody drifts. The people of Aurora's Rest wake in the early light and go directly to the forest zone and pick up where the night left off, because the alternative is standing still and standing still is something the roar made impossible.

The platforms get finished first. Cyrus walks each one personally, testing boards under his full weight, checking joins, reinforcing anything that moves more than he allows. Smart2004 follows him with the rope supply and closes the gaps that Cyrus flags without needing instruction. The result by mid-morning is not elegant but it is solid, a network of elevated positions tied into the major trunks of the forest zone, connected by rope lines and accessible by ladders that Galaxy helped anchor deep enough to hold weight under urgency.

Toad tests the supply hoists under full load, running the heaviest crate from the ground to the lowest platform three times to check for slip. It holds. He marks the rope with a piece of cloth at the correct tension point so anyone using it in the dark can find the limit by feel.

The work has a different character from previous days. Quieter. More focused. Nobody questions Rally mally and nobody debates method and nobody stops to discuss what the roar meant. The roar is inside all of them now and it has done something to the social architecture of Aurora's Rest that leadership and philosophy did not manage, which is to make disagreement feel like a luxury the camp has collectively decided it cannot afford right now.

Golden watches this with the particular irritation of someone whose leverage depends on fracture and who is watching the fracture seal.

Galaxy moves through the forest zone in patterns that have stopped being patrols and started being something more territorial, wider circuits, slower pace, head lower, the raptor attention distributed outward toward the walls rather than inward toward the camp. He respects Rally mally's positioning and adjusts his routes around the work zones without being directed. When Lord Kalameet crosses his circuit path they acknowledge each other with a brief stillness and then continue.

Kiwi sits at the base of his tree with a cloth and a piece of charcoal and draws.

Not the Maze. Not corridors. He is mapping the forest acoustics, marking which trunks reflect echo and which absorb it, which clearings produce standing waves, where the waterfall sound arrives cleanest and where it arrives distorted. He cannot explain why he is doing this and nobody asks him.

space fan watches from a distance.

His recovery speed is wrong. She knows it is wrong. She has seen Quigg venom progression before and Kiwi's trajectory does not follow it. He should be weaker. He is not. He is mapping sound by instinct and his hands are steady and his eyes carry the peripheral sharpness that appeared in Night 9.

She does not know yet whether this is good.

SomeDucks works without speaking. The Ninnginni-shaped absence in the camp has become structural now, load-bearing in its own way. He reaches once toward a conversation that is not there anymore and then goes back to work.

Polaris organizes elevated sleeping assignments without asking permission, pairing people by function rather than preference. Rally mally reviews the structure, then approves it without changes.

The elevator begins at midday.

The ascent sound arrives first. Familiar vibration, then distortion. Metal strain. A prolonged mechanical scream that is louder than any previous delivery. The sound travels through the stone and into bone.

Kiwi stops drawing.

His breathing shortens. His eyes close.

space fan is beside him immediately.

"I am fine," he says.

"I know."

The platform takes longer than any previous ascent.

When it reaches the surface, it is empty.

No person. No body. No visible note.

Rally mally signals stillness. The camp holds position.

Toad approaches first at Rally's nod.

He checks the platform.

Then looks up.

The crates are mounted inside the elevator housing.

Fire.

Torches wrapped in treated cloth. Fuel containers sealed with wax. Ignition kits prepared with precision. Fire-resistant fabric. Flare devices with dual-color casings.

The camp stands around the opened crates in silence.

Toad speaks first. "This is controlled burn equipment."

Duccarian watches the fuel. "It is not help."

SomeDucks asks, "Then what is it."

"A variable," Duccarian says. "Introduced deliberately."

Polaris adds, "Or something we are meant to want."

Rabbit turns a flare device in his hands. "They sent weapons."

Golden replies, calm and measured. "They sent tools. What we make of them defines us."

Rally mally issues storage orders immediately. Fuel separated. Ignition restricted. Burn zone designated.

Golden notices he is not consulted.

The afternoon reorganizes itself around fire.

Cyrus marks controlled burn lines. Smart2004 calculates spread vectors. Toad establishes fuel protocol.

Galaxy refuses to approach the containers closer than ten feet. He circles them, agitated, instinct overriding memory.

Rabbit notices.

He does not find comfort in what that implies.

Kiwi studies his acoustic map. The pattern is wrong. Sound behaves as though shaped externally.

space fan listens to his explanation and does not dismiss it.

By evening Aurora's Rest looks different.

Platforms in trees. Fire in storage. Twenty people inside the walls.

One of them missing.

Duccarian stands by the waterfall holding Frodge, who has trembled since the elevator scream.

"They gave us fire," he says quietly. "In places like this, fire has usually been used against each other."

He watches the water.

"Or against something we have not seen yet."
