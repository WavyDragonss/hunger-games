[[DAY]]
DAY 11 — THE TOOLS OF WAR

[[NARRATIVE]]
Morning arrives to a camp that is already moving.

Nobody waits for Rally mally to call headcount. Nobody drifts. The people of Aurora's Rest wake in the early light and go directly to the forest zone and pick up where the night left off, because the alternative is standing still and standing still is something the roar made impossible.

The platforms get finished first. Cyrus walks each one personally, testing boards under his full weight, checking joins, reinforcing anything that moves more than he allows. Smart2004 follows him with the rope supply and closes the gaps that Cyrus flags without needing instruction. The result by mid-morning is not elegant but it is solid, a network of elevated positions tied into the major trunks of the forest zone, connected by rope lines and accessible by ladders that Galaxy helped anchor deep enough to hold weight under urgency.

Toad tests the supply hoists under full load, running the heaviest crate from the ground to the lowest platform three times to check for slip. It holds. He marks the rope with a piece of cloth at the correct tension point so anyone using it in the dark can find the limit by feel.

The work has a different character from previous days. Quieter. More focused. Nobody questions Rally mally and nobody debates method and nobody stops to discuss what the roar meant. The roar is inside all of them now and it has done something to the social architecture of Aurora's Rest that leadership and philosophy did not manage, which is to make disagreement feel like a luxury the camp has collectively decided it cannot afford right now.

Golden watches this with the particular irritation of someone whose leverage depends on fracture and who is watching the fracture seal.

Lysrix moves through the build zone with the efficiency she brings to everything, not directing, not building exactly, but occupying the spaces between tasks where things stall and clearing them before anyone notices they were blocked. She repositions a rope coil that was about to be stepped on. She hands Smart2004 the correct anchor pin before he asks for it. She watches Rally mally the way she always watches him, reading the gap between what he says and what his posture holds, and what she reads today she does not share with anyone.

JIKOSU works alongside Lord Kalameet through the morning, the two of them assigned to reinforcing the heaviest platform supports with the metal brackets from the Day 10 supply crates. JIKOSU moves with the careful precision of someone still accounting for healing ribs, but his hands are steady and his pace is consistent. Lord Kalameet works beside him without commentary, the physical task converting itself into progress with the same quiet efficiency his presence always converts into stability. At one point JIKOSU pauses and looks at the Maze doors with an expression that has nothing casual in it. Lord Kalameet follows his gaze and holds the same silence.

Neither of them says what they are thinking.

Nightmare is on the perimeter.

He has been there since before the others woke, walking the wall line in slow deliberate circuits, stopping at intervals to press his palm flat against the stone and hold it there, reading vibration. Nobody assigned him this. He assigned himself to it with the particular certainty of someone who has stopped waiting for instruction and started operating on a different frequency entirely. His pupils in the morning light are wrong in the way they were wrong last night, too wide for the brightness, too fixed. He has not eaten since last night. If this bothers him, his body does not show it.

Rabbit watches him complete one full circuit and then looks away without saying anything.

Galaxy moves through the forest zone in patterns that have stopped being patrols and started being something more territorial, wider circuits, slower pace, head lower, the raptor attention distributed outward toward the walls. He respects Rally mally's positioning and adjusts his routes around the work zones without being directed. When Lord Kalameet crosses his circuit path they acknowledge each other with a brief stillness and continue.

Trxty works silently near the medic hut through the morning, not on fortification, not on supply work. He is making something small and compact, assembled from scraps of the supply crates with a focus that excludes everything around him. Nobody asks what it is. By midmorning it is finished and he pockets it and goes back to carrying rope for the platform teams without explaining himself.

Kiwi sits at the base of his tree with a cloth and a piece of charcoal and draws.

Not the Maze. Not corridors. He is mapping the forest acoustics, marking which trunks reflect echo and which absorb it, which clearings produce standing waves, where the waterfall sound arrives cleanest and where it arrives distorted. He cannot explain why he is doing this and nobody asks him.

space fan watches from a distance.

His recovery speed is wrong. She knows it is wrong. She has seen Quigg venom progression before and Kiwi's trajectory does not follow it. He should be weaker. He is not. He is mapping sound by instinct and his hands are steady and his eyes carry the peripheral sharpness that appeared in Night 9.

She does not know yet whether this is good.

SomeDucks works without speaking. The Ninnginni-shaped absence in the camp has become structural, load-bearing in its own way. He reaches once toward a conversation that is not there and then goes back to work with the tight expression of someone who has decided that the gap is something he will have to carry rather than fill.

Polaris organizes the elevated sleeping assignments without asking permission, pairing people by function rather than preference, weighting physical capability toward the outer positions and placing the recovering and vulnerable toward the center. She presents the plan to Rally mally as a completed structure rather than a proposal.

Rally mally reviews it and approves it without changes.

The elevator begins at midday.

The ascent sound arrives first. Familiar vibration, then distortion. Metal strain. A prolonged mechanical scream that is louder than any previous delivery, the mechanism under a load it was not designed to carry comfortably, the sound traveling through the stone and into the bones of everyone in Aurora's Rest.

Kiwi stops drawing.

His breathing shortens. His eyes close. The specific acoustic signature of mechanical distress finding the part of his nervous system that the venom recalibrated and lodging there.

space fan is beside him immediately.

"I am fine," he says, without opening his eyes.

"I know," she says. She stays anyway.

JIKOSU, across the camp, goes still when the scream peaks. Not the reaction of someone startled. The reaction of someone who recognizes a sound and has feelings about recognizing it. He returns to his bracket work without looking at anyone.

Trxty watches JIKOSU's stillness.

The platform takes longer than any previous ascent.

When it reaches the surface it is empty.

No person. No body. No visible note.

Rally mally raises his hand and the camp holds position. Nobody approaches. The empty platform sits in the center of Aurora's Rest like a question that has not finished being asked.

Toad approaches first at Rally's nod, checking the platform surface, the edges, underneath. Then he looks up.

The crates are mounted inside the elevator housing, secured above the platform surface. He calls it out and the unloading begins in controlled silence.

Fire.

Torches wrapped in treated cloth. Fuel containers sealed with wax. Ignition kits assembled with the precision of something prepared carefully rather than gathered. Fire-resistant fabric in bolts. Flare devices with dual-color casings, the meaning of the color difference not yet understood.

The camp stands around the opened crates and nobody speaks for a moment.

Toad speaks first. "This is controlled burn equipment. Prepared. Not improvised."

Duccarian watches the fuel containers. "It is not help."

SomeDucks asks, "Then what is it."

"A variable," Duccarian says. "Introduced deliberately. We are meant to use this."

Polaris looks at the flare devices. "Or meant to want to."

Rabbit turns one in his hands. "They sent weapons."

Golden replies, calm and measured, with the specific warmth of someone whose operating theory just received supporting data. "They sent tools. What we make of them is still ours to decide." He pauses. "The Maze just told us what is coming. That is not a threat. That is a courtesy."

Lord Kalameet looks at Golden for a moment with the expression he has used before on him, the one that contains a full sentence without requiring words. Golden receives it and does not push further.

Rally mally issues storage orders immediately. Fuel separate from torches. Ignition kits locked until he authorizes. Burn zone designated away from the platforms.

He does not look at Golden when he gives the orders.

Golden notices.

Nightmare comes off the perimeter when the fire supplies are laid out and stands at the edge of the circle looking at the fuel containers. He does not look afraid of them. He looks at them with the specific attention of someone running calculations nobody else has access to. Then he steps back and returns to the wall line without speaking.

Lysrix watches him go and says nothing.

The afternoon reorganizes itself around fire. Cyrus marks the controlled burn lines in the clearing. Smart2004 calculates spread vectors accounting for the platform positions and wall vent directions. Toad establishes the fuel protocol and designates himself its keeper.

Galaxy refuses to approach the containers closer than ten feet. He circles them in tight agitated patterns, instinct overriding the human memory of what fire means, the animal part of his cognition louder than everything else on this particular subject. He does not interfere with the storage operation. He simply will not come close.

Rabbit watches this and thinks about what it means that the most physically capable being in Aurora's Rest is afraid of something that fits in a sealed container.

He does not arrive at a comforting conclusion.

JIKOSU sits near the medic hut entrance through the afternoon, watching the burn zone preparation with an expression that is doing several things at once. At one point Trxty sits beside him and the two of them are quiet together for a long stretch, neither speaking, both watching the same things with eyes that are working on different problems.

space fan checks hollow Camila and Mainthemainstar in the medic hut. Both sedated. Both stable. She adds their status to her private record and returns to Kiwi's tree.

Kiwi has gone back to the acoustic map. He traces a pattern on the cloth and says, without looking up, "The sound does not match the structure. The tree placement should produce one resonance pattern. It produces another."

space fan looks at the cloth. "Something is shaping it from outside."

Kiwi looks at her. "Yes."

Neither of them has a framework yet for what that means. But neither of them dismisses it.

As the light begins its cooling shift, Aurora's Rest looks different from how it looked eleven days ago. Platforms in the forest canopy. Fire stored and locked. Twenty people inside the walls, one missing, two unconscious, one mapping sounds that should not exist, one walking the perimeter with pupils that do not respond to brightness correctly.

Duccarian stands at the waterfall edge in the late afternoon holding Frodge, who has trembled intermittently since the elevator scream and has not fully settled. He speaks quietly.

"They gave us fire," he says. "In a place made of stone. Against creatures that are partly mechanical." He watches the fall. "The only thing fire is reliably useful against in here is each other."

He holds Frodge a little closer.

"Or something they have not shown us yet."

[[NIGHT]]
NIGHT 11 — THE FALL OF AURORA

The Maze doors do not close.

That is the first thing.

The light cools the way it always does, the artificial sky dimming through its programmed sequence, and the camp moves through the familiar motions of night preparation, platforms assigned, fire positions checked, supplies hoisted, the vertical architecture of their eleven-day survival clicking into its evening configuration. And then the light finishes cooling and the Maze doors stay open and the silence that follows is not the silence of safety.

It is the silence of a rule that no longer applies.

Rally mally is standing at the edge of the forest zone when it happens. He does not move. He looks at the open doors and the dark corridor beyond them and the cold air breathing outward into Aurora's Rest and he understands what the note meant. He understood when he read it. He has been building toward this moment for two nights and the arrival of it does not surprise him and does not make it easier.

Duccarian appears at his shoulder without being called. He looks at the open doors. He looks at Rally mally. Neither of them speaks. There is nothing to say that the open doors have not already said.

The air pressure changes.

It is subtle at first, a density shift, the feeling of atmosphere compressing slightly as though something enormous is displacing it from the corridor side. The forest platforms creak. Not from weight. From the change in pressure moving through the wood grain. The waterfall sound distorts briefly, the smooth fall catching a frequency interference that warps it into something uneven before it corrects.

Then the first roar comes.

Distant. West corridor. The same territorial signal as Night 10 but different in character, less announcement and more the sound of something that has finished deciding and is now moving. The camp reacts with the controlled fear of people who have been trained by eleven days to understand what sounds mean.

The second roar comes faster than anyone expected.

Closer. Much closer. South wall. The ground vibration arrives ahead of the sound, a seismic precursor that travels through the platform supports and into the feet of everyone standing on elevated wood. Several people grab rope lines for stability. The waterfall deflects again, the basin surface breaking into concentric rings.

Then the ground-shaking resonance.

Not a roar this time. Movement. Something so large that its footfall registers as geology rather than biology, each impact a compression wave that Aurora's Rest receives from its foundations upward. The trees shiver. Loose stones on the orchard path jump once and settle.

Toad grips the fermentation pot without thinking and holds it still and looks at the Maze entrance and does not let go.

space fan is already at the medic hut, checking hollow Camila and Mainthemainstar, her face locked into the clinical neutrality she uses when the situation has exceeded the vocabulary of normal response.

SomeDucks climbs to the first platform and looks toward the corridor and sees only dark and mist and the faint mechanical glow that the Maze produces from its interior lighting, distant and cold and blue-white.

Then the figure appears in the doorway.

Walking slowly.

Not a Quigg. Not C A R L. A person, upright, moving at a human pace through the corridor mist, emerging into the peripheral glow of the Maze entrance with the particular unhurried quality of someone who has no reason to hurry.

The figure steps into the faint light of Aurora's Rest.

Ninnginni.

The camp processes this in layers, relief arriving first because the human brain defaults to hope before it defaults to reason, and then confusion because the Maze doors are open and he is walking out of them at night, and then the particular cold dread of a pattern completing itself, the pressed grass and the broken marker and Galaxy stepping back from the wall and Frodge going still at the far corner and the absence that was never quite the right shape for a disappearance.

Ninnginni stands at the Maze threshold and looks at the camp.

He does not speak.

Cyrus sees him from the second platform and his body makes the decision before his mind finishes the argument. "Ninnginni." He moves toward the ladder. "He is alive. He is right there."

Rally mally says, sharp and immediate, "Cyrus. Stop."

Cyrus does not stop.

He is already on the ground, already crossing the clearing toward the entrance, the eleven days of Ninnginni's absence and the structural weight of the missing rhythm and the simple human fact of seeing someone alive who should have been dead doing what eleven days of discipline cannot entirely prevent.

Then Cyrus sees what is behind Ninnginni.

One Quigg.

Then two more, flanking.

Then the corridor mouth filling with movement, mechanical tails raised, wall-crawling arms extended, bodies low and disciplined, a formation rather than a hunt. They emerge in pairs and take positions on either side of Ninnginni with the ritualistic precision of an honor guard, twelve of them total, arranged in a corridor of living bodies that points from the Maze entrance toward Aurora's Rest.

They do not attack Ninnginni.

They do not look at him.

They are not hunting him.

They are escorting him.

The understanding arrives in the camp the way the worst understandings arrive, not as a revelation but as a confirmation of something already known that nobody had been willing to finish knowing.

Cyrus is three meters from the entrance when the Quigg on the left side of the formation moves.

It does not hesitate. It does not choose. It simply reaches.

Cyrus makes a sound that is not a word, a single syllable of pure surprise, and then the Quigg pulls and the sound stops and Cyrus is gone into the formation and the formation closes and Aurora's Rest stands in the silence of what just happened.

Ninnginni does not react.

He does not look at the place where Cyrus was. He does not flinch. He takes one step back, placing himself deeper inside the formation, his body choosing the corridor over the camp with the quiet finality of a decision that was made long before tonight. That single step back says everything his silence has been carrying since Day 9.

space fan's hands stop moving in the medic hut. She does not make a sound.

Rabbit grabs the rope line beside him and holds it and looks at the formation and at the place where Cyrus was and his face does something that burns off the last remaining trace of the person who arrived on the elevator nine days ago.

SomeDucks, on the first platform, watches Ninnginni step back into the formation and understands that the thing he has been reaching for in the silence is not coming back. Not because it was taken. Because it walked.

Then the shadow arrives.

It comes from above the west wall first, a darkness that is too large and too deliberate to be shadow. The camp looks up because the camp has run out of directions to look that are not toward something terrible.

Then C A R L rises over the wall.

Not over it the way something leaps. Over it the way something ascends, mechanical forelimbs finding purchase in the upper stone, the body hauling itself to the crest with the deliberate power of a siege organism that does not hurry because hurrying implies doubt. It crests the 90 meter wall and pauses there for a single moment, silhouetted against the artificial sky, the Carnotaurus mass of it and the mechanical organic fusion of bone and engineered joint occupying the wall line like a landmark that has always been there and has only now decided to move.

Then it drops into Aurora's Rest.

The landing sends a shockwave through the ground strong enough to knock people from their feet. The trees shiver to their roots. The waterfall basin sloshes.

And on its back, seated with the ease of someone who has ridden this particular thing for a very long time, a human figure.

Schwalbe.

Not large. Not armored. Not monstrous in the way the situation demands a monster. A person, sitting on the back of the apex entity of the Maze with the composed and absolute authority of someone for whom this is not a dramatic entrance but simply an arrival. The face is not visible in the distance and the dark. Only the posture. The specific posture of someone who has been watching Aurora's Rest for eleven days from above and has now decided to come down.

The scale shock moves through the camp like the roar did, a full-body recalibration of what is real and what is possible. Several people make sounds they will not remember making. Several do not make any sound at all.

Duccarian holds Frodge against his chest and looks at C A R L and then at the figure on its back and says, very quietly, "There it is."

Rally mally does not look at Schwalbe.

He looks at his camp.

"Retreat to the platforms," he says. His voice is the voice he uses when there is no room for anything except the next action. "Ignite the fire lines. Defensive formation. Move."

They move.

The fire lines go first. Toad has been planning the controlled burn zones since the fuel arrived and the ignition sequence takes him forty seconds, running the lines with torch in hand, the pre-laid fuel catching in clean succession, the designated burn zones coming to life in controlled walls of orange that cut the clearing into sectors and funnel the corridor between the entrance and the tree structures into a narrowed approach.

The Quigg formation responds to the fire with the tactical intelligence of something that has been directed rather than instinctive, pulling back from the burn lines, redistributing around the flanks, probing for gaps with the patience of a coordinated unit.

SomeDucks is on the upper platform with a bow that arrived in one of the supply crates and has been waiting for a use that everyone hoped would not come. He nocks and draws with the steadiness of someone who has decided that the platform is where he lives now and what is below it is the problem.

The first Quigg finds a gap in the fire line on the eastern flank and moves through it fast and low.

SomeDucks' arrow catches the mechanical tail joint, not the kill shot but the disabling one, the Quigg dropping its tail and losing half its mobility in a single impact, the creature dragging and pivoting and screaming.

Nightmare drops from the platform above.

Earlier in the night, before the doors failed to close, Rabbit had noticed something about Nightmare that he had not known how to name. The way his pupils sat wrong in the firelight, too wide, too fixed. The way he had crossed the platform in three steps that should have taken five. The way he pressed his palm flat against a hot platform beam to test its stability and left it there two seconds longer than anyone would leave a hand on something that hot, then removed it without looking at the mark.

Whatever Night 9 did to Kiwi, something else entirely has been happening to Nightmare. Not venom. Something chosen or endured or discovered in the private hours when the camp was not watching. The platform drop confirms it. He does not land like a person absorbing a fall. He lands like something that has recalibrated what falling costs.

What happens to the crippled Quigg is not a fight. It is the removal of something from the board with a violence that goes beyond what the situation strictly requires, beyond what the situation explains. He uses his hands and his knees and the edge of the platform support and by the time it is over the Quigg is not moving and Nightmare is standing over it with his arms at his sides and his breathing barely elevated and his expression containing something that was not there before Night 9 and that nobody in Aurora's Rest has had the chance to ask him about.

He looks across the clearing at Ninnginni, standing inside his formation corridor.

"TRAITOR." The word comes out of him like something structural breaking. Not rage exactly. The specific sound of eleven days of trust arriving at its invoice.

Ninnginni does not look up.

Nightmare stands there for one more second and then turns back toward the fight because the fight is what is real and what is happening and grief and betrayal are currencies Aurora's Rest has never been able to afford to spend in the moment they are generated.

The camp kills three Quiggs in the first phase. SomeDucks from the platform, Nightmare on the ground, Galaxy moving through the fire-lit clearing with the specific territorial efficiency of something that has been waiting for a context in which its nature is not a problem but a solution. Galaxy kills the third, driving it into the burning ground cover and holding it there until it stops.

Three Quiggs dead. Nine total engaged.

Then Schwalbe moves.

C A R L does not charge. It walks. The difference matters. A charge implies urgency. What C A R L does is the movement of something that has already calculated the outcome and is simply proceeding to it, each footfall a seismic event, the camp's fire lines bending in the displacement of its passage, the platform supports vibrating at its approach.

Schwalbe points.

The gesture is small and specific and directed at Nightmare, who is standing between C A R L and the eastern platform with a piece of broken support beam in his hands and the expression of someone who has decided that standing here is the last decision he will make but has decided it completely.

C A R L obliges.

It is not dramatic. That is the terrible part. There is no contest, no moment of opposition, no pause in which something different could have happened. C A R L simply arrives where Nightmare is standing and then Nightmare is no longer standing there. The sound it makes is absorbed by the roar that accompanies the motion and the fire and the screaming of the remaining Quiggs pressing the flank and by the time anyone has processed what just happened the space where Nightmare was is empty and C A R L is still moving.

SomeDucks is on the platform and he saw it and he draws another arrow and releases it at C A R L and it strikes true and bounces off the mechanical plating and falls into the fire below and SomeDucks looks at the arrow and at C A R L and nocks again because there is nothing else to do.

Rally mally shouts, "UP. EVERYONE UP. NOW."

The survivors climb. Rope ladders, platform edges, hand over hand with the fire below and the Quigg formation pressing the flanks and C A R L moving through Aurora's Rest with the unhurried certainty of a system completing a process.

The fire begins to spread beyond its designated zones.

Not because the planning was wrong. Because two Quiggs on the eastern flank rupture the sealed fuel storage with their tails during the flanking push, the containers splitting and pouring accelerant across the ground in a quantity the burn barriers were not designed to contain. Then C A R L's movement crushes the western burn boundary entirely, the packed earth fire-break flattening under its footfall, the controlled wall of flame becoming uncontrolled in the thirty seconds it takes the ground to absorb the load. The camp's tactical fire becomes a terrain fire and the difference is everything.

Schwalbe surveys the burning camp from C A R L's back.

Then he looks at the fortress cluster in the northern trees, the strongest construction in Aurora's Rest, the one Cyrus designed and Smart2004 optimized, where Kiwi had been placed because it was the most stable and most removed from the ground.

Schwalbe looks at it for a moment with the expression of someone reviewing an item on a list.

Then he directs C A R L.

The platform structure takes the impact at its base supports. All three anchor trunks. C A R L hits them in sequence with the mechanical efficiency of demolition rather than violence, each strike precise, each trunk giving way in the order that causes the maximum structural cascade. The platforms do not fall dramatically. They come apart, section by section, the rope lines snapping under load, the boards separating, the whole elevated structure descending in pieces through the tree canopy and into the burning clearing below.

Smart2004 is gone before the second trunk gives way.

Lord Kalameet holds the rope line until the third trunk goes and then the rope is not connected to anything and the platform is not connected to anything and the drop is real and immediate and absolute.

Kiwi does not make a sound.

He was awake. He saw it coming. He was watching Schwalbe direct C A R L toward the structure with the post-venom clarity that had been his anomaly for two days and in the seconds before impact his eyes were open and oriented and reading the pattern the way he had always read patterns, completely and too late.

He hits the ground and does not move.

The medic hut goes next.

The eastern fire line has failed entirely, the ruptured fuel spreading the burn into the uncontrolled areas, the orchard catching along its fence line, the heat reaching the medic hut structure from two directions simultaneously. The structure reaches the point where it is no longer a structure.

hollow Camila and Mainthemainstar are still sedated.

They do not wake up.

Aurora's Rest burns.

The orchard rows blacken. The waterfall basin fills with ash that the recirculating water pulls through its system, the endless fall now carrying grey. The four sad trees that were the original clearing, the trees they named on Day 1 when naming things was a way of claiming that the place could belong to them, catch and burn. Dave the Tree goes last, the largest of them, holding for longer than the others before the fire reaches something it cannot negotiate past.

The camp kills three more Quiggs during the collapse phase. Toad with a burning piece of structural timber. Galaxy with the force of something that has nothing left to lose. Rabbit with a fire-hardened tool and the specific focus of someone who has decided that surviving means continuing to act regardless of what is happening around the action.

Six Quiggs total dead from camp action. Six withdraw with C A R L.

C A R L does not pursue the survivors into the upper canopy. This is not mercy. The upper canopy is not the objective. The objective was Aurora's Rest, the settlement, the civilization the Peeps built inside the cage, the village that Duccarian named and the beer that Toad brewed and the orchard rows and the grave at the eastern edge where pinkfml's waterfall stones are now buried under ash.

The objective was everything they made.

And it is gone.

Schwalbe looks at the burning clearing from C A R L's back, at the survivors clinging to the upper canopy with smoke rising through the trees around them. He looks at this for a moment with the expression of someone reviewing a completed process. Then he directs C A R L back toward the Maze entrance.

The Quigg formation withdraws with them.

Ninnginni is already gone, absorbed by the Maze interior before anyone tracked where he went. The mystery of his absence resolves into the mystery of what he is now, which is a different and heavier question that the survivors do not have the capacity to examine tonight.

The Maze doors close.

For the first time tonight, the Maze doors close.

The fire continues without them.

The camp remains in the upper canopy through the remainder of the night, spread across the highest surviving platforms and the strongest remaining rope lines, looking down at what Aurora's Rest has become. space fan sits in the junction of two major branches with her legs wrapped around the trunk and her medical kit in her lap and she counts the people she can see and she counts them again.

Twelve.

Plus Frodge, who is pressed against Duccarian's chest in the tree above her and has not made a sound since the roar.

Twelve where there were twenty this morning.

Rally mally is on the highest remaining platform, standing, watching the fire below finish its work. He does not sit. The note that was in his pocket burned with everything else when he had to abandon the shelter line and climb. It does not matter. He has not needed to read it again since the first time.

JIKOSU climbs to Rally mally's platform near the end of the night, moving carefully on ribs that are still healing, and stands beside him and looks at the burning clearing below.

He says, "I am sorry."

Rally mally looks at him. "For what."

JIKOSU looks at the fire. He does not answer immediately. When he does his voice has the specific flatness of someone choosing their words around the shape of something they will not say directly. "For what is coming next." A pause. "I have seen him before. Schwalbe. Not here. Before here."

Rally mally holds that.

He turns back to the fire and says nothing, because there is nothing to say to something that sounds like foreknowledge and confession arriving in the same sentence, not tonight, not while Aurora's Rest is still burning.

Rabbit sits in the fork of a high branch and looks at his hands in the firelight and thinks about Cyrus reaching for something that turned out not to be the thing it looked like. About the gap between what we run toward and what we arrive at. About eleven days of Aurora's Rest and what it cost and what it bought.

He decides that is a calculation for a different night.

Toad holds what is left of the fermentation pot. He got it out before the fire reached the shelter line, grabbing it in the evacuation, carrying it up the rope ladder with one hand and the camp's survival with the other. The beer inside it survived. He does not know what to do with that.

SomeDucks is in the tree nearest the waterfall zone and watches the ash fall into the basin water and thinks about Ninnginni stepping back into the formation, one step, deliberate, unhurried, the specific body language of someone who has made their choice and is no longer pretending otherwise. The framework he is building for this does not have a clean shape yet. He builds it anyway. It is the only thing he knows how to do with something that hurts this much.

Galaxy crouches on the widest platform, blood from two new wounds mixing with the older ones, and looks at the burning clearing with his head low. Not grief. Something beyond grief, the response of something that has lost its territory. He does not look at the Maze doors. He looks at the fire.

Duccarian holds Frodge.

The duck is still. Breathing. Eyes open in the dark and the smoke, the animal sensing system tuned to something the humans around it have not finished processing yet.

Duccarian presses his hand over the duck's back and looks at what remains of Aurora's Rest below him and says, very quietly, into the smoke and the fire and the dark, "We built something real. They had to burn it to make us stop believing in it."

He pauses.

"That means it was real enough to matter."

Frodge makes a small sound. The first sound since the roar.

It is not much.

But it is something alive in the dark, and right now that is the only measurement that applies.

[[NIGHT]]
Night 11 ends with Aurora's Rest in ashes and twelve survivors in the canopy above it.

The waterfall still runs.

It runs through the ash and the smoke and the absence of everything that was built around it, the same endless engineered fall into the same dark basin, indifferent to what was lost, indifferent to what it witnessed, indifferent in the way that only something that was never alive can be indifferent.

The Maze doors are closed.

C A R L is in the corridors.

Schwalbe has shown his face.

And somewhere in the stone labyrinth, Ninnginni is walking away from something he chose and toward something he has not yet finished choosing, and the camp that trusted him is in the trees above the ruin of everything they built, and the distance between those two facts is the full width of what this place does to people.

Aurora's Rest is dead.

The Maze is the battlefield now.

And the twelve people in the canopy have until morning to decide what that means for who they are going to become.
