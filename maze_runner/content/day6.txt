[[DAY]]
DAY 6 — THE WATER THAT NEVER ENDS

[[NARRATIVE]]
Day 6 arrives like the place is pretending to be generous.

The light is warmer. The air feels less metallic. For a few minutes, it almost tricks them into forgetting Night 4 and the sound of Axlot disappearing.

Rally mally does headcount before anyone can drift.

Kiwi answers and keeps his eyes on the Maze doors.
Lysrix answers and watches who looks tired enough to make a mistake.
Ut answers and checks the stone seam near the entrance like it might bite.
janhk. answers and rubs his fingers like he still feels last night in his bones.
Ninnginni answers with a joke that dies halfway.
space fan answers while already checking bandages and food packets.
-MAD$- answers while counting supplies under his breath.
Duccarian answers calmly, as if calm is armor.
Nightmare answers without looking up.
Cyrus answers and keeps glancing toward the orchard rows.
SomeDucks answers quietly, thoughtful, like he is listening to the whole place at once.
Popcorn Rya answers and looks up at the artificial sky like she wants it to apologize.
pinkfml answers in a whisper and stays close to Dave the Tree.
Doomfox answers and forces his shoulders back, trying to look braver than he feels.
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) answers without words, tapping the cage bars once, then scratching a quick symbol in the dirt that looks like a corridor corner.

The ground elevator rises.

Everyone steps back automatically now, giving it space like it is a wild animal.

A new Peep steps out.

Toad.

He blinks hard, takes in the orchard, the forest, the cage, the tired faces, and then exhales like he expected this nightmare and hates being right. He looks at Rally mally first, as if leaders have a smell.

Rally mally speaks gently, but firm. “Name.”
Toad answers. “Toad.”
Rally mally nods. “You follow rules and you eat. Then you help.”

A crate rises with him.

Tools. Clean cloth. A small metal pot. A bundle of twine. A packet of dried something that smells faintly sweet and sharp. And a note written on scrap paper in neat handwriting.

Toad reads it aloud because nobody wants mysteries in their pockets anymore.

It says
Make something worth waiting for.

Ninnginni squints. “That is either hopeful or evil.”
Duccarian replies, “Both can be true.”
Nightmare mutters, “Finally. Someone useful.”

Toad sets the pot down carefully. “If we get real water, I can make something that tastes like a reason to breathe.”
SomeDucks tilts his head, curious. “You mean tea.”
Toad smiles once. “I mean beer.”
Popcorn Rya laughs despite herself, shocked by the sound.
pinkfml looks up, a flicker of almost warmth in her eyes, then she hides it again.

Rally mally does not let the mood float too high.

He points toward the Maze doors. “Lever run today. We unlock the third zone. We do it fast and we do it smart.”

Nightmare steps forward immediately. “Send me.”
Rally mally shakes his head. “No. I need you here if something comes early. You watch the doors.”
Nightmare’s expression turns annoyed, then satisfied, like being trusted with control is still a win.

Rally mally chooses the runner team.

Ut for mapping and caution.
janhk. for seams and traps.
Kiwi for patterns and instincts.
Doomfox because he is fast, and he has been trying too hard to prove he belongs.

space fan speaks before they leave. “If anything stings or clicks, you stop. You come back.”
Lysrix adds, “If the corridor loops, mark it. Do not chase momentum.”
Duccarian says quietly, “The Maze does not punish bravery. It punishes assumption.”
-MAD$- hands Ut a strip of cloth. “Tie this at turns. We do not lose people in stone.”
SomeDucks watches the team and says softly, “Do not let pride pick your footsteps.”
Toad just nods once, absorbing everything like he stores it for later.

They go.

The Maze entrance is open wider today.

The air inside is cold enough to bite.

Ut leads at a measured pace, counting steps, listening for the faintest shift in stone. janhk. stays half a pace behind, eyes on seams, hands hovering but never touching. Kiwi watches corners and floor patterns like he is reading a language nobody taught him. Doomfox brings up the rear, breathing hard, trying not to be the weak link.

They pass two silent pressure plates that janhk. spots by the way dust refuses to settle on certain stones.

Doomfox whispers, “How do you see that.”
janhk. whispers back, “Because the Maze wants you to miss it.”

They reach a junction that should lead somewhere new.

Instead, it loops.

Ut stops instantly. “We just walked the same wall mark twice.”
Kiwi nods. “It is feeding us circles.”
Doomfox swears under his breath. “So we are lost.”
janhk. points at a seam line that is thinner than the others. “Not lost. Redirected.”

They take the seam line corridor.

The stone changes texture. Slightly damp. Slightly smoother. Like a path meant to be used.

Then they find the lever.

It is not hidden in an alcove like the others. It is almost casual, bolted into the wall with a grip that looks too clean.

Ut stares at it. “Too easy.”
Kiwi whispers, “That is the warning.”
janhk. checks the floor. Checks the mount. Checks the surrounding seam. “No pressure plate. No needle slot. But the handle housing is strange.”
Doomfox steps forward anyway, chest rising like he is about to win something. “I can do it. I will do it.”

Ut hesitates, then nods once. “Pull and step back.”
Kiwi adds, “Do not stand under it.”
janhk. says, “Hands only. No body weight.”

Doomfox grips the lever.

It goes down easily.

Way too easily.

A clean alarm tone rings through the Maze, confirming unlock.

And then the lever bites.

A mechanism snaps inside the wall.

Two metal spikes punch out low and fast, catching Doomfox in both legs.

He does not even scream at first. He just makes a broken sound of surprise, like his brain did not believe pain could happen that quickly.

Then he screams.

Ut grabs him under the arms immediately and drags him back from the lever housing. Kiwi yanks Doomfox’s collar hard to keep him from falling forward into the mechanism again. janhk. stares at the spikes and whispers, “It was the lever. It was always the lever.”

Blood pours down Doomfox’s calves.

Doomfox gasps, “I pulled it. I pulled it. We did it.”
Kiwi’s voice is tight. “Yes. Now we leave.”
Ut looks down the corridor. “Fast. Before something hears this.”

They half carry, half drag Doomfox back.

Every step is a choice between speed and keeping him from passing out.

The Maze feels louder now. The corridors echo their breathing. The stone seems to hold the smell of blood like it is pleased.

They reach the entrance as the light outside looks a fraction cooler, not night yet, but shifting.

Back in Aurora’s Rest, the alarm tone reaches the camp and the third zone unlocks.

The smog barrier clears.

And behind it, a roar of water appears.

A waterfall.

Not a normal one.

It drops too far, too clean, too perfect, a sheet of water pouring down into a dark basin that should fill and overflow but never does. The fall looks infinite, like the water is falling into nowhere.

Cyrus stares. “That is impossible.”
Duccarian murmurs, “No. That is engineered.”
Toad whispers, almost reverent, “That is water.”
-MAD$- says, “That is survival.”

The runner team bursts out with Doomfox between them.

space fan runs to them and drops to her knees, hands already moving. “Lay him down. Now.”
Lysrix kneels too, calm hands, sharp eyes. “Pressure first. Then clean.”
pinkfml flinches at the blood and forces herself to stay, to watch, to not run.
Popcorn Rya grips her own sleeves and whispers, “He did it. He did it and it still punished him.”
Ninnginni mutters, “The Maze does not do gratitude.”
Nightmare watches the blood and looks almost annoyed that the pain is not happening to him.

Ut explains fast. “Lever was easy. Trap fired after confirmation. Spikes through both legs.”
janhk. adds, “Mechanism was inside the lever housing. No external tell.”
Kiwi looks at Rally mally. “If it is easy, it is bait.”

Rally mally nods once, jaw tight. “We still needed it. We still paid.”

They stabilize Doomfox as best they can with what they have.

space fan binds tight.
Lysrix keeps Doomfox talking so he does not drift.
Doomfox shakes, trying to smile through pain. “At least I was useful.”
SomeDucks answers softly, wise and sad, “Being useful should not cost you your legs.”
Toad sets the pot down beside the shelter line and looks at the waterfall zone like it is the first real promise they have seen.

They go to the water.

Not everyone. Rally mally sends a controlled group.

Toad because he knows what water means.
-MAD$- because he counts risk.
Cyrus because he builds.
SomeDucks because he watches quietly and sees what others miss.
Nightmare because Rally mally does not trust leaving him alone.
Kiwi and Ut because patterns matter.
janhk. because mechanisms matter.

space fan and Lysrix stay with Doomfox. pinkfml stays near them too, forced into stillness by the reality of blood and bandages.

The waterfall zone is beautiful in the way a display is beautiful.

The water crashes down in a smooth sheet, and the basin below is crystal clear, but the surface never rises. The flow never changes. The sound never shifts.

Kiwi kneels near the edge and watches foam patterns. “It loops.”
Ut nods. “Recirculating.”
Duccarian, from a distance, calls softly, “Infinite fall effect.”
Cyrus rubs his temples. “We are living inside a machine.”

Toad kneels and tastes the water with a careful finger. “Clean.”
-MAD$- says, “We boil anyway.”
Toad nods. “Of course.”

Then SomeDucks spots something near the basin edge.

A small duck.

Not a rubber toy.

A real living duck, damp feathers, bright eyes, sitting in the spray like it belongs there. Like it has always belonged there.

SomeDucks crouches slowly, hands open, posture calm, the way you approach something that can choose to leave.

Ninnginni whispers, “Is that real.”
Cyrus whispers back, “Do not scare it.”
Nightmare watches, confused and irritated by anything that looks gentle.

The duck waddles closer to SomeDucks on its own.

SomeDucks looks stunned, then quietly pleased, like he has been handed a fragile truth.

He speaks to it softly. “Hello.”
The duck tilts its head.
SomeDucks smiles. “You are the first thing here that looks like it wants to live.”

Kiwi watches the duck, then watches SomeDucks. “It picked you.”
SomeDucks answers, almost embarrassed. “Or I picked it by not trying to own it.”

Toad laughs once, surprised. “The philosopher of Aurora’s Rest has a disciple.”
SomeDucks glances at him. “Do not start calling me that.”
Ninnginni grins. “Too late.”

SomeDucks lifts the duck gently, expecting it to flap away.

It settles.

He holds it like it is a piece of sanity.

Then he pauses, like naming it would be a promise.

Ut says, “Name it.”
janhk. adds, “Names matter.”
Kiwi says, “Names make things real.”

SomeDucks looks down at the duck, thinking too hard, making suspense out of silence.

Cyrus offers, “Pond.”
Ninnginni offers, “Dave Two.”
Nightmare mutters, “Bait.”
Toad smiles. “Blessing.”
-MAD$- says, “Do not waste time. Water first.”
SomeDucks ignores them all, still staring at the duck like it is listening.

Then he speaks, certain.

“Frodge.”

The duck makes a small sound that is not quite a quack, and it stays tucked against his arm like agreement.

Kiwi exhales, the tension in his shoulders dropping a fraction. “Frodge is a terrible name.”
SomeDucks answers calmly, “Frodge is perfect.”
Lysrix, from far back near the shelters, cannot hear the name, but she sees the way Kiwi briefly looks like a normal person again.

They bring water back in containers they improvise.

Toad immediately starts planning.

He sets the pot over a safe heat source, boils water, then starts assembling a crude fermentation setup with cloth, twine, and whatever sugars they can spare from rations and orchard scraps.

He speaks quietly as he works. “Give me time and I can make morale.”
Duccarian replies, “Morale is a resource.”
Nightmare mutters, “Beer does not stop Quiggs.”
Toad answers, “No. It stops people from breaking before Quiggs arrive.”

As the day pushes toward the light cooling, the camp becomes busy and tense.

Cyrus reinforces shelters.
-MAD$- organizes water rations like it is gold.
Ut and janhk. begin marking safe approach paths to the waterfall zone.
Kiwi keeps glancing at the Maze doors.
SomeDucks keeps Frodge close, speaking to it under his breath like the duck is a confession booth.
Popcorn Rya sits beside pinkfml and tries to talk about anything else.
pinkfml nods, not really hearing, eyes stuck on Doomfox’s bandaged legs.
space fan checks Doomfox’s pulse again and again.
Lysrix stays beside her, steady, refusing to leave the injured.

Doomfox tries to lift his head. “I can still walk.”
space fan answers firmly, “No. You can still live.”
Lysrix adds, “If we keep you here.”
Doomfox whispers, desperate, “If they close the doors and I am not inside, I am done.”

The light cools faster than expected.

The Maze doors begin their closing cycle.

Stone shifts. A grinding roar rolls outward.

Rally mally shouts, “Everyone inside the shelter line. Now. Doomfox stays central.”

They try to drag Doomfox farther from the entrance, but he fights, panic turning his strength ugly.

“I will not be left behind.”

And then something moves near the Maze doors.

Not inside Aurora’s Rest.

Just there, at the stone lip, where the corridor breathes cold air into the clearing.

A Quigg appears early.

It crawls low, wider than a normal raptor body, arms long enough to climb the corridor walls with ease. Its mechanical scorpion tail clicks as it moves, heavier, slower in pure speed, but terrifyingly agile in short bursts.

It does not step onto the grass.

It prowls the threshold, sniffing, head tilting, listening to heartbeats like they are music.

Everyone freezes.

Nightmare whispers, almost admiring. “Early.”
Duccarian murmurs, “Schedule glitch.”
janhk. breathes, “Do not move.”
Ut’s voice is barely there. “It is smelling the blood.”

Doomfox makes a small sound of terror.

The Quigg turns toward him like the sound is a hook.

It lunges without crossing into Aurora’s Rest, stretching forward from the stone lip, claws catching Doomfox’s legs with brutal efficiency.

Doomfox screams and grabs at the dirt, trying to pull himself back.

Rally mally surges forward one step, then stops.

Because the Quigg is still not on the grass.

Because the doors are grinding.

Because one wrong move could pull more than Doomfox into the corridor.

space fan shouts, “Doomfox hold on.”
Lysrix grabs space fan’s arm hard. “No. You do not trade.”
Kiwi’s eyes go wide, helpless rage and fear fighting in his face.
SomeDucks clutches Frodge tight, whispering like prayer.
Cyrus takes a half step, then freezes, understanding too late that courage cannot beat geometry.

The Quigg drags Doomfox backward into the corridor.

Doomfox reaches out with shaking hands, fingernails scraping stone, leaving pale lines.

He locks eyes with Rally mally for one second.

Then the corridor shadow eats him.

The Quigg prowls the threshold for a few more seconds, tail clicking, head turning as if it is considering whether the grass is worth the risk.

It does not cross.

The Maze doors slam shut.

The sound is final.

The camp does not move for a long time.

space fan sinks to her knees, shaking, staring at where Doomfox was.
Lysrix sits beside her, face tight, voice low. “You did everything you could.”
Kiwi looks at the closed doors like he wants to burn through stone with will.
Nightmare stands still, satisfied in the worst way, like the night proved him right about everything.
Duccarian watches the group and says nothing, because nothing fits.

SomeDucks finally speaks, voice quiet, wise and broken. “The Maze takes who it wants, and it teaches us to call it logic.”

Frodge shifts in his arms, warm and alive, and for a second the softness makes the loss hit harder.

[[NARRATIVE]]
Day 6 ends with water in their hands and blood in their mouths.

They have a waterfall that never runs out.

They have a duck named Frodge.

They have beer starting to breathe in a cloth wrapped pot.

And they have one fewer Peep.

The doors stay shut.

The walls stay high.

Nothing climbs out.

But something inside is learning exactly how to reach them anyway.
