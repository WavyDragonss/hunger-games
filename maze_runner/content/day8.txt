[[DAY]]
DAY 8 — THE BEACONS

[[NARRATIVE]]
Nobody waits to be called this morning.

The light has barely shifted from night-grey to something warmer when the camp is already awake, already upright, already looking at the same thing.

Three pillars of light rise from somewhere deep inside the Maze.

They are thin and pale and precise, visible above the 90 meter walls like something the structure is pointing at itself. Not emergency signals. Not warnings. They are calm and vertical and completely certain of themselves, the way only engineered things can be certain, and they have been there since before the first Peep opened their eyes.

Kiwi stands at the edge of the shelter line and studies the beams without blinking.

Ut is beside him, map cloth already in hand, and his expression goes through several calculations before he says anything. When he does speak his voice is quiet and tight. "They are far apart."

Kiwi nods. "Different sectors."

Ut looks at the spacing between the beams, measuring angles against the wall line. "If the corridors have shifted since yesterday we do not have clean routes to any of them."

Rally mally calls the meeting before the camp has finished orienting.

He stands in the center of Aurora's Rest and speaks without preamble. "Three crates. Three beacons. The note said retrieval required by today. We go in and we come back out with all three." He pauses. "We do not split up in the Maze. We move as one group and we take the beacons in order."

JIKOSU is standing at the edge of the meeting circle.

He looks like someone who slept badly inside a sedative and woke up angry about it. The colour is not fully back in his face and space fan has already told him twice to sit down, but he is standing, and he is dressed, and he is looking at the Maze doors with the particular expression of someone who has decided what they are going to do regardless of what anyone recommends.

space fan checks his pulse one more time with two fingers and exhales through her nose. "Stable. No irregularity. Whatever they used wore off clean." She looks at Rally mally. "He is functional."

JIKOSU says, "I am going."

Rally mally studies him for a moment. "You stay with the group. You do not take point. If you go down someone carries you."

JIKOSU nods once.

Rally mally names the team.

Himself. Kiwi. Ut. janhk. Cyrus. Rabbit. Nightmare. -MAD$-. SomeDucks. Galaxy. JIKOSU.

Staying at camp: space fan, Lysrix, Toad, Duccarian.

space fan hands out the bandage supply without being asked. Lysrix watches the team assemble with an expression that does not show what it is counting. Toad checks the fermentation pot once and then sets his hands flat on his knees and watches the doors. Duccarian holds Frodge against his chest and says nothing.

The duck watches the team prepare with its small bright eyes.

janhk. crouches at the Maze entrance and reads the seam lines before anyone steps through. He moves his hand along the floor without touching it, feeling air pressure changes, watching dust settle. "Fresh configuration. The shift last night changed at least two corridor junctions from what I mapped."

Ut marks the map cloth with new notation. "We navigate by beacon position. We correct as we go."

Kiwi looks at the three pillars of light above the walls. "The first one is north-west. Closest."

Rally mally steps through the doors first.

The Maze swallows them.

The air inside is colder than it has any reason to be. The corridors are quiet in a way that is different from normal quiet, not empty but waiting, the way a held breath is different from an absence of breathing. No Quigg screams. No distant grinding. Just the sound of eleven sets of feet on stone and the faint mechanical hum that lives inside the walls of everything in this structure.

Ut leads navigation. Kiwi tracks corners and wall markings. janhk. moves at the edge of the group, eyes on the floor seams, hands hovering without touching. Rabbit stays close to Rally mally, reading the group and reading the corridors in equal measure. Galaxy moves at the rear, her footfalls heavier than the rest, the mechanical joints of her new body producing a faint rhythmic click that the Maze seems to absorb without echo.

JIKOSU keeps pace. Barely. But he keeps it.

The first beacon is located faster than expected, the corridor route relatively clean, one loop that janhk. catches immediately and redirects around. The light pillar is visible at the end of a long straight passage, rising from a metal crate bolted to the floor with thick anchor pins.

The crate is unmarked. Functional. Cold to the touch.

-MAD$- checks the anchor pins while janhk. checks the surrounding floor. "No plate pressure on the crate itself," janhk. says. "But the corridor behind us—"

He does not finish the sentence.

The Quiggs come from the side passage.

Two of them, fast and low, mechanical tails raised and clicking, arms wide to use the full corridor width. They move the way things move when they have been waiting and are glad the waiting is over.

The first one reaches the group before anyone has fully turned.

Galaxy hits it from the side with the full weight of her body, shoulder into the Quigg's ribcage, driving it into the wall hard enough that the stone cracks. The Quigg screams and thrashes and Galaxy gets both forearms around its neck and locks. The sound it makes when the spine goes is too loud for the corridor.

The second Quigg drives toward JIKOSU who is the slowest in the group and clearly reads as the weakest target.

Nightmare is already moving. He hits the Quigg's flank with both arms, redirecting rather than stopping, and Rabbit comes in from the other side with a piece of the rope-secured tool handle and drives it into the joint where the mechanical tail meets the body. The Quigg screams and backs off, tail dragging, and retreats into the side passage.

Silence returns.

Galaxy stands over the first Quigg's body and breathes hard, flanks heaving. A claw mark runs across her left shoulder, dark and deep. She does not acknowledge it.

-MAD$- opens the first crate.

Bandages. Rope, stronger than anything they have managed to make themselves. Food rations, dense and unfamiliar. Two flare sticks, bright orange, sealed in wax paper.

He catalogues everything in under thirty seconds and distributes the load.

Kiwi is already looking at where the Quigg came from. "It was inside the side passage before we arrived." He looks at the crate. "It was waiting here."

SomeDucks says quietly, "The crate is the bait. The supplies are the hook."

janhk. nods. "Every beacon is defended. We expect it now."

Rally mally picks up the flare sticks and hands them to Rabbit. "You carry these. You use them only if I say."

Rabbit pockets them without comment.

They move to the second beacon.

The path is harder. The corridors narrow in two places, forcing single file, and the Maze shifts once while they are inside it, a grinding shudder that lasts only seconds but changes the wall alignment at a junction and costs them a detour. Ut and janhk. argue in low tense voices about the correction.

"The beacon is east-southeast from our entry point," Ut says, studying the cloth. "The junction shift moved us thirty degrees off. We compensate left at the next opening."

janhk. shakes his head. "The left opening has a seam pattern I do not trust. Pressure variance in the floor. We go right and loop."

"The loop adds distance and time."

"The left passage may add a spike through someone's foot."

Ut looks at the seam. He looks at the cloth. "Right," he says.

janhk. was correct. The left passage, visible as they pass its entrance, contains a pressure plate mechanism so poorly concealed that the stone around it is slightly newer than everything else, a replacement plate installed without care, or installed with the intent of being found too late.

The second beacon chamber is wider than the first. High ceiling. Four wall tunnels branching upward at angles.

That detail registers in the wrong part of Kiwi's mind at the wrong moment.

He says, "The tunnels—"

Three Quiggs drop from the ceiling.

They come down the wall tunnels fast and diagonal, claws finding purchase on the stone, tails high, and they hit the group from three separate angles before anyone can fully orient.

Galaxy takes two of them.

She moves through the chamber with the particular violence of something that has stopped measuring its own strength, catching the first Quigg mid-drop with both forearms and driving it into the floor, then spinning to intercept the second before it reaches SomeDucks. The second one fights harder and gets its tail around Galaxy's right forearm before she breaks the joint with one sharp rotation and slams the creature against the nearest wall.

The third Quigg goes for the back of the group.

It moves through a corridor junction, fast and precise, and it finds Ut and janhk. on the turn.

What happens is quick.

Ut sees it first and pushes janhk. sideways out of the main line of attack. The Quigg takes Ut instead, claws finding his chest and side, the mechanical tail whipping. janhk. comes back immediately, hands and body, trying to pull Ut back from the creature's grip, and for a moment it looks like he might. But the corridor is narrow and the Quigg is braced and janhk. takes the tail strike full across his head and goes down hard against the wall.

Neither of them gets up.

Rally mally reaches the junction and sees it and stands with his jaw locked and says nothing for three full seconds.

The Quigg is already retreating down the side corridor with whatever it took from the encounter. It does not pursue the group further.

Nightmare reaches janhk. first. He checks the pulse the way someone checks a pulse when they already know what they are going to find, with focus rather than hope.

He stands up.

He says nothing.

Rally mally looks at Ut and then at janhk. and then at the remaining nine people in the corridor. He does not let the grief arrive yet. There is no room for it in the Maze. He closes it somewhere behind his eyes and speaks.

"We take the second crate and we finish this."

-MAD$- opens the crate with hands that are not entirely steady.

Medical supplies, better than anything space fan has worked with since they arrived. Several strange injector pens, clear casing, no label. Energy packs, dense and light. Metal climbing hooks with teeth designed for stone.

SomeDucks crosses the chamber and steps over a floor seam without looking down.

He does not notice the needle.

It catches the back of his ankle, thin as a wire, retracting back into the floor before anyone could have seen it. He takes two more steps and pauses, frowning, and looks down at his boot. There is a small dark mark at the seam of the leather.

He says nothing.

He picks up a section of the supply load.

They leave Ut and janhk. in the corridor because there is nothing else they can do and everyone knows it and nobody says it aloud. Rally mally is the last one out of the junction and he looks back once before he turns the corner.

The walk to the third beacon is long and wrong in a way that is difficult to name.

The group is quieter. Galaxy is bleeding from the shoulder and the forearm but moving steadily, redistributing her weight to compensate. JIKOSU keeps pace with visible effort, jaw set, not asking for help and not getting it unrequested. Rabbit watches the ceiling junctions every time they pass under one.

Kiwi watches SomeDucks.

He noticed the pause at the floor seam. He noticed the mark on the boot. He has not said anything yet because SomeDucks has not said anything yet and the Maze is not a place where you can stop moving to have a conversation.

But he watches.

Twenty minutes into the walk SomeDucks begins sweating.

It is cold in the Maze. The sweat makes no sense. He wipes his face once and keeps moving. Then he stumbles slightly on a flat section of corridor and catches himself on the wall and stands very still for a moment.

Kiwi touches his arm. "You were stung."

SomeDucks breathes. "I know."

"When."

"Second chamber. Floor seam."

Kiwi looks at Rally mally. Rally mally looks at SomeDucks. SomeDucks is already looking forward, down the corridor, toward the third beacon.

"I can finish," SomeDucks says. His voice has a new edge to it, tight and slightly wrong, like something is pulling at the frequency of it.

Rally mally looks at him for a long moment. "You tell me the second it gets worse."

SomeDucks nods once and keeps walking.

The third beacon chamber is the largest they have seen.

High vaulted ceiling. Four corridors entering from different angles. The crate sits in the center on a raised stone platform, beacon light rising from a mechanism underneath it, thin and pale and calm.

Suspiciously calm.

Nobody moves toward it immediately.

janhk. would have checked every seam in this room before allowing a single step forward. janhk. is not here.

Kiwi does it instead, moving slow and careful around the room's perimeter, eyes on the floor. He finds one pressure plate near the left entry corridor and marks it with a chalk line from the supply pack. He finds nothing else.

-MAD$- approaches the crate.

He opens it.

Advanced medical supplies, more injector pens, compact tools, a signal flare with a pull-ring trigger.

Then the wall tunnel above the far corridor opens and a Quigg drops.

This one is larger than the others. Wider across the body, longer forelimbs, the mechanical tail thicker and slower but more deliberate in its movement. It lands in the chamber and orients immediately on JIKOSU, reading the group with the efficiency of something that has been watching for a while and has already chosen.

It crosses the chamber fast.

JIKOSU raises his arms but the Quigg hits him low and drives him into the stone platform hard enough to crack the edge. The mechanical tail rises, positioning, and the chamber goes very still.

Then the Quigg stops.

Its head tilts.

The tail holds its position, trembling slightly with suspended intent, and the Quigg's eyes go somewhere else, somewhere inside a frequency none of the Peeps can hear. The whole body shifts like it has been spoken to.

Then it retreats.

Fast and absolute, back up the wall tunnel, the sound of its claws on stone fading into the corridor system above.

Nobody moves.

Nightmare says it into the silence. "C A R L."

The name lands the way it always does in Aurora's Rest, not as information but as weight.

Kiwi's voice is barely there. "It called them off."

Cyrus looks at the wall tunnel. "Why."

Rally mally says, "We do not wait to find out. Move."

They move.

The return through the Maze is fast and ugly, the group carrying supply load and wounded and the specific fear of something large enough to command Quiggs with a sound. The corridors feel different on the way back, not the same stone and configuration but the same corridors read through different knowledge, the knowledge that something in this structure is not just hunting but directing, that the Quiggs are not the system, they are the instruments.

Galaxy keeps moving. Her shoulder is dark with dried blood and the forearm wound has slowed but not closed. She carries the heaviest crate without being asked and does not slow the group.

Rabbit and Rally mally keep JIKOSU between them, matching his pace.

SomeDucks walks at the edge of functional. Kiwi stays beside him and does not touch him and watches his hands for trembling. By the time the Maze entrance is visible ahead SomeDucks is breathing through his teeth and his expression has contracted into something tight and almost unrecognizable.

They come out of the Maze with the light still above the walls.

The doors begin grinding closed behind them.

space fan reaches them before they have fully crossed the threshold.

She takes one look at the group and starts triaging without speaking. Galaxy to the flat surface near the hut. JIKOSU seated against the shelter wall. She touches SomeDucks' face with both hands and his eyes focus on her with difficulty and she turns to Lysrix immediately. "The injectors. From the crate. Now."

Lysrix opens the second crate and finds the pens.

Clear casing. No label. No instructions.

SomeDucks sits down on the ground because his legs have decided the decision for him. His hands are shaking now, full tremors, and he says something that is not words exactly, just the framework of a sentence with the meaning removed.

Toad stands back and watches with his hands at his sides and says nothing because there is nothing useful to say.

Duccarian holds Frodge and watches SomeDucks and his face does something quiet and terrible.

space fan holds one of the injector pens and turns it in her hands. She reads everything on the casing that can be read, which is nothing. She looks at SomeDucks and looks at the pen and looks at Rally mally.

Rally mally says, "Use it."

She administers it.

The camp waits.

SomeDucks' eyes close. His breathing slows in a way that makes everyone in the vicinity stop breathing in response. Then it steadies. Slowly. The tremors reduce. The tightness in his face releases by degrees until it becomes just exhaustion, ordinary exhaustion, the kind a person can survive.

He does not wake up immediately.

But he breathes.

space fan sits back on her heels and puts both hands flat on her thighs and stares at the ground for a moment.

"He is stable," she says.

Nobody cheers. Nobody speaks. The information lands in a camp that is too tired and too reduced to do anything with relief except hold it quietly.

Rally mally stands in the center of Aurora's Rest with two dead Peeps in the Maze and three supply crates retrieved and a camp that came back from something it barely survived. He looks at the Maze doors, now fully closed, stone against stone, the structure sealed and still.

Lysrix says, from beside the supply crates, "We retrieved everything it asked for."

Rally mally nods.

Cyrus says, slowly, like the thought arrived while he was thinking something else, "It let us leave."

The camp is quiet.

Kiwi says, "The Quigg in the third chamber had JIKOSU. It stopped."

Rabbit says, "C A R L called it back."

"Yes."

"Why."

Nobody answers that.

Nightmare looks at the Maze doors. "Because it could have finished us and it chose not to." He pauses. "Which means today was not the point."

The realization moves through the camp without being spoken into a full sentence. The Maze sent three beacons. It wrote a note with a time limit. It guarded the crates with Quiggs it could have sent in larger numbers. It let C A R L appear and then retract. It let eleven Peeps walk in and nine walk out and then it closed its doors and went quiet.

It did not try very hard.

SomeDucks is unconscious near the shelter line with his chest rising and falling in slow even increments.

Galaxy sits against the wall of her hut with space fan working on the forearm wound, head back, eyes open, watching the artificial sky.

JIKOSU sits against the shelter wall and looks at the ground and does not speak.

Toad goes to the fermentation pot. He checks the seal. He sits down beside it.

Duccarian stands near the orchard edge and looks at the two stones at the eastern row that were not there a day ago. He holds Frodge and the duck is still against him, warm and alive and entirely unaware of what happened in the corridors today.

He says, quietly, to no one in particular, "The Maze let us succeed."

Lysrix answers, from a few feet away, "Yes."

"That is not kindness."

"No," Lysrix says. "It is not."

Duccarian looks at the Maze doors. "It wants us to know we can survive inside it. So that we go back."

Nobody disagrees.

The light holds above the walls.

The waterfall runs.

The orchard rows are still. The beer breathes in the pot. The supply crates sit open and sorted by -MAD$- with the methodical focus of someone who needs to do something with his hands.

Three crates retrieved. Two Peeps in the Maze who are not coming back. One Peep breathing through a needle that had no instructions. One Peep who arrived yesterday and has already been inside the corridors.

One creature that can stop a hunt with a sound.

And the doors are closed.

And the Maze is quiet.

And that, more than anything else today, is the thing that nobody knows what to do with.

Day 8 ends with the Maze doors closed and nine Peeps in Aurora's Rest instead of eleven.

The beacons are dark now.

The supply crates are sorted.

The injector pen worked.

And somewhere in the corridors, two Peeps are not coming home, and the thing that could have stopped it chose not to, and the thing that could have finished the rest chose to wait.

Aurora's Rest has water and food and rope and medicine and a duck and a raptor and a fermentation pot three days from being ready.

And the Maze is watching all of it.

And it is not finished yet.
