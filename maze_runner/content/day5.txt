[[DAY]]
DAY 5 — AFTER THE ROAR

[[NARRATIVE]]
The day starts with silence that feels earned and stolen at the same time.

No one talks for a while. Not because they have nothing to say. Because last night taught them what noise can cost.

The orchard looks the same. The forest looks the same. The cage looks the same.

Everything looks normal except the people.

Rally mally does headcount anyway, voice rough. “Names.”

Kiwi answers.
Lysrix answers.
Ut answers.
janhk. answers.
Ninnginni answers.
space fan answers.
Toad answers.
Duccarian answers.
Nightmare answers.
Cyrus answers.
SomeDucks answers.
Doomfox answers.
pinkfml answers, barely.
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) does not answer with a voice. He presses his claws into the dirt inside the cage and scratches three short lines, then taps the bars once like he wants them to understand he is still present.

Rally mally stares at the scratched lines and nods as if the gesture counts. “Good.”

Nobody says Axlot’s name.

They all know where he went.

They all know what the night sounded like afterward.

They all pretend that not saying it makes it less real.

Toad sets their morning like a schedule. Tools checked. Food counted. Water packets divided. “We do not fall apart today. We build smarter.”
Ninnginni rubs his face and looks toward the Maze doors. “I hate how normal the light is. Like the place doesn’t care.”
Duccarian answers quietly, “It doesn’t.”
Nightmare adds, “That’s the point.”

Rally mally walks everyone to the Maze entrance but keeps them several paces back. The doors are closed now, stone slabs sealed tight like nothing ever moved.

janhk. kneels and runs a fingertip along the seam. “No new scratches.”
Ut points to the ground just outside the doors. “Wrong.”
There are marks. Shallow gouges, like claws tested the stone. And one long scrape line that looks like metal teeth dragged across rock.

SomeDucks swallows. “That was outside.”
Kiwi whispers, “It came close.”
Lysrix doesn’t blink. “It was listening.”

space fan turns away first. “Not today. Today we live.”

They spend the day making Aurora’s Rest feel less like a patch of grass and more like a place that can last.

The orchard becomes a work zone. Seed rows get measured and marked. Cyrus and Ut dig shallow channels in the dirt, not for water yet, just to prepare. janhk. makes simple signs and sticks them at the edge of each hectare, so nobody walks into the wrong zone in a panic later.

Doomfox takes to labor like he’s trying to earn his right to be here. He hauls wood from the forest hectare and doesn’t complain once.
Nightmare watches him and says, “Good. Use the new guy until he breaks.”
Doomfox hears it and keeps hauling anyway.

pinkfml sits under Dave the Tree for a while, then forces herself up and joins space fan near the shelter line. She doesn’t speak much. She just helps tie vine cord and keeps her hands busy so her mind can’t replay screams.

Lysrix does rounds without calling them rounds. She checks who sits with who. Who avoids who. Who looks at the Maze doors too long. She stops by Rally mally at the tool pile and speaks low. “If we keep pretending last night didn’t change us, it will change us worse.”
Rally mally nods. “Then we make rules.”

He gathers them again midday, when their bodies insist it should be noon even if the sky refuses to prove it.

Rally mally speaks in a calm voice that sounds like it hurts. “New law. When the light cools, nobody goes near the Maze doors. Nobody. We do not tempt early hunters. We do not test the night.”

Cyrus asks, “What about lever runs.”
Rally mally answers, “Day only.”

Nightmare smirks. “So we’re prisoners in daylight too.”
Rally mally meets his eyes. “We’re alive in daylight. That’s enough.”

Duccarian watches the group accept the law, then says softly, “Rules make people predictable.”
Kiwi answers, “Predictable keeps us alive.”
Lysrix adds, “Until the Maze adjusts.”

They return to the cage.

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) is calmer today. Not calm like fine, calm like exhausted. He scratches into the dirt again.

This time he draws a rectangle. Then a line. Then another line branching.

It looks like a corridor map.

janhk. kneels closer, careful. “You remember the Maze.”
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) taps the dirt twice, then scratches one sharp X in the corner.

Ut’s voice is quiet. “He’s telling us where something is.”
Kiwi whispers, “Or where something hunts.”

space fan looks between them. “We can’t use him like a tool.”
Rally mally answers, “We can listen to him like a person.”

SomeDucks sits nearby, still sore from the scratch, watching GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) with a mix of fear and guilt. “I’m sorry I freaked out.”
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) blinks slowly at him, then scratches one short word.

OK

That tiny word hits harder than any scream. It is proof that the monster in the cage is not a monster.

It is a Peep who got changed.

The rest of the day is small work and smaller emotions.

They improve the shelters. They reinforce the cage. They tie a warning cloth around the elevator zone so nobody walks close by accident. They drag a heavy log in front of the Maze doors at a safe distance like it will matter if something comes early.

As the light cools, the group slows down. Heads turn toward the Maze doors without permission.

Nothing opens.

Nothing crawls out.

But the orchard trees creak anyway. The forest stays too quiet. The walls feel like they are waiting.

Rally mally says, “Inside. Now.”

They go inside the shelter line and eat what little they have. Nobody enjoys it. But they eat, because starvation makes stupid decisions.

The day ends with a strange kind of victory.

No deaths. No new traps. No new notes.

Just a stronger camp, a clearer law, and a cage that holds a friend who might become their best advantage.

And far beyond the sealed doors, the Maze stays silent in the way a predator stays silent.

Because it already ate last night.

And it will want to eat again.

[[NIGHT]]
NIGHT 5 ACCEPTING THE RAPTOR

The light cools again and the camp responds before fear can argue.

Rally mally stands and claps once, sharp. “Inside the shelter line. No one goes near the Maze doors. Not even to listen.”

Nobody questions it now.

The orchard rows become shadows. The forest hectare becomes a black wall. The Maze doors stay closed like they are pretending nothing lives behind them.

janhk. sets fresh notch marks near Dave the Tree, hands steady but eyes tired. Ut resets the tool pile position by habit, turning the camp into a shape he can recognize in the dark. Toad counts food packets and speaks quietly to Cyrus about ration order like numbers can keep the night away.

Doomfox drags a heavy log closer to the shelter line and pauses, breathing hard. “We should reinforce the wind side.”
Ninnginni answers, voice low, “There is no wind. There is just vibes.”
Duccarian watches the orchard shadows and murmurs, “Vibes kill.”
Nightmare smiles faintly, the kind that never reaches warmth. “Good. Then we stay sharp.”

SomeDucks sits with his bandaged arm tucked in, staring at the cage. The scratch still looks small, but it did what fear always does. It made him imagine what could happen if the cage ever opened wrong.

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) shifts inside the cage, restless but not raging. His tail taps dirt once, then stops. He scratches the ground again, slower than before.

He writes three crude words.

HELP. CARRY. GOOD.

space fan reads it out loud like it matters that he is heard. “He wants to help carry. He says he can.”
pinkfml stares at the words for a long moment, then whispers, “It’s still him.”
Lysrix nods once. “It is him. The body changed, not the intention.”
Kiwi stays silent, eyes on the cage latch, then on Rally mally, waiting for the call.

Rally mally takes a long breath. “We do it controlled. Rope line. Two handlers. No crowding.”

Nightmare scoffs softly. “We are letting a raptor loose at night.”
Rally mally answers, “We are letting a Peep work. And if you spook him, you will answer to me.”
Nightmare’s smile widens a fraction. He says nothing, but he steps back.

Ut and Cyrus take the rope. Toad holds the cage door mechanism and keeps tools out of reach like it is sacred. SomeDucks moves away on instinct, then forces himself closer anyway, swallowing his fear.

space fan opens the cage.

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) steps out.

He moves low and smooth, weight balanced like a machine built for quiet. The sight still hits everyone wrong, but the way he glances at their faces hits different. It is familiar. It is asking permission without words.

Lysrix speaks softly, careful. “Slow. You help, then you go back.”
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) blinks once, then lowers his head slightly like agreement.

They give him the log Doomfox could barely move.

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) grips it in his longer arms and lifts it like it is nothing. The log rises clean off the dirt, stable, no wobble. He carries it to the shelter line and places it down gently, almost politely.

Doomfox stares. “Okay. That’s… useful.”
Ninnginni lets out a breath that sounds like relief. “He’s a forklift with feelings.”
pinkfml covers her mouth, then actually laughs once, small and shocked. She flinches after like laughing might trigger punishment. Nothing happens. The world stays cold.

SomeDucks steps forward slowly and holds out a bundle of vine cord. “Can you… take this too.”
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) leans in, careful around the injured arm, and takes it without touching skin. He carries it across and sets it down beside the log. Then he scratches two quick letters in the dirt with a claw.

OK

SomeDucks’ shoulders drop like a weight got removed.

They keep him working for a short time.

Ut guides paths so nobody gets clipped by tail swings. Cyrus points where beams should go. Toad hands materials in strict order. Rally mally watches every movement with hard focus. Lysrix watches faces, tracking who is loosening and who is tightening.

Kiwi stays near the edge of the shelter line, listening for the Maze.

Because the night is never only camp.

The first Quigg scream arrives faint and distant, bouncing through stone corridors like the Maze is amplifying it on purpose. It is sharp enough to make pinkfml freeze. It is followed by the mechanical rasp of movement somewhere far away, claws and metal tail scraping in bursts.

space fan lowers her voice instantly. “Back. Quiet.”
Rally mally nods once. “Enough work. Cage. Now.”

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) pauses like he is listening too. His head tilts toward the Maze doors, then back to Rally mally. He obeys immediately, carrying the last beam to the shelter line, then returning to the cage without being forced.

Nightmare watches him go back in and mutters, “He acts trained.”
Duccarian answers quietly, “Or he acts loyal.”
Lysrix looks at Nightmare. “Does that bother you.”
Nightmare shrugs. “Nothing bothers me. That’s why I’m still here.”

The cage closes. Toad secures the latch twice. Ut ties the rope line away from hands. Cyrus sits down and presses his palm into the dirt like he is grounding himself.

The Quigg screams grow louder for a few minutes, then fade, then return from a different direction. It sounds like patrols scraping along walls and dropping down corridors, never seen, always heard. The camp stays still, learning the difference between noise that passes and noise that hunts.

Doomfox whispers, “They sound close.”
janhk. whispers back, “Sound lies in stone corridors. Do not trust it.”
Kiwi murmurs, “The Maze wants us to move at the wrong time.”
Rally mally answers, barely above breath, “Then we do not move.”

SomeDucks watches the dark orchard rows and clenches his bandaged arm with his other hand. pinkfml leans against Dave the Tree and breathes in slow counts, copying space fan’s calm like it is a lifeline. Ninnginni sits with his back to the shelter wall, eyes open, refusing to close them.

The Maze shifts again later, a long grinding wave that makes the orchard trunks creak. The sound rolls under them like a train they cannot see. Dust falls from seams along the Maze doors even though the doors do not open.

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) scratches one long line in the dirt inside the cage, then another, then stops. It looks like a warning mark. Or a map. Or both.

Lysrix watches the claw marks and whispers to Rally mally, “He is remembering things we cannot.”
Rally mally nods, eyes hard. “Then we keep him alive.”

The night ends without sleep, just fatigue and listening.

No new screams close enough to force a run.

No sign of C A R L.

Only the quiet understanding that tonight they did something that mattered.

They made space for a changed friend.

And they did it while the Maze screamed in the dark like it hated that they still had the ability to choose.
