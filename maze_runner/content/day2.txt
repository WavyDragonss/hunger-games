[[DAY]]
DAY 2 — THE FIRST UNLOCK

[[NARRATIVE]]
Day 2 arrives too fast, like the place skipped the part where people recover.

The light is warm again, almost friendly, but nobody trusts it now. Everyone looks tired in the same way, like the night didn’t end so much as it loosened its grip for a moment.

The elevator cage sits low in the tear, still and waiting.

Then it moves.

A slow grind, cables tightening, the metal frame rising like a throat clearing itself.

Rally mally is already on his feet. “Back up. Nobody crowds it.”

The cage rises into view and stops with a soft mechanical click.

And someone is inside.

Doomfox stumbles out, blinking hard, pale as if he’s been in a box for a long time. He looks at the fake grass, the walls, the people, and his face shifts through confusion into the kind of fear you feel when you realize you’re late to a disaster.

Doomfox takes a breath that shakes. “Where am I.”

Before anyone can answer, supplies rise with him, packed in neat crates like a delivery.

Food. Real food. Not much, but enough to matter.

Tools wrapped in plastic. One hoe. Three axes. A small bag of seeds that rattles when moved.

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) stares like he’s seeing treasure. “Tools.”

-MAD$- is already counting. “Three axes. One hoe. Seeds. Food. That means we’re meant to build.”

Duccarian steps closer to the crates without touching. “It means they expect progress.”

Lysrix watches Doomfox more than the supplies. “Do you remember arriving.”

Doomfox shakes his head. “No. I woke up in the elevator. It was moving.”

Kiwi’s eyes narrow. “So the elevator is the intake. It brings Peeps in.”

Nightmare’s voice is flat. “And it can bring anything.”

Rally mally gestures toward the supplies. “We’ll split this cleanly. Nobody hoards. We need a routine.”

janhk. crouches by the axe head, checks the metal, then the handle. “These are basic. Not cheap. Not meant to break fast.”
Ut looks at the seeds like they’re a promise he doesn’t trust. “No animals, no insects, no birds. Just grass and trees. What are we planting for.”
Ninnginni answers quietly. “Hope. Or bait.”

A low groan rolls through the Maze.

Not the night shifting.

A daytime sound.

A warning that the “still” part is ending.

Then a section of wall opens further than it ever did on Day 1. The slit becomes a clear entry corridor, dark beyond the threshold.

Everyone goes silent.

Rally mally doesn’t let it turn into panic. “Okay. The Maze is open. We do not all rush it. We send three.”

He scans the group once, choosing speed and steadiness over bravado.

“Ut. Kiwi. Axlot.”

Axlot grins like this is finally what he wanted.
Ut nods once, already focused.
Kiwi swallows and looks at Lysrix for half a second, then back to the corridor. “Got it.”

Rally mally adds the real rule. “In and out. Mark what you see. If something feels wrong, you turn around.”

Nightmare scoffs. “Coward rule.”
Rally mally doesn’t look at him. “Alive rule.”

They step through the threshold.

The air changes instantly. Colder. Cleaner. The sound of Aurora’s Rest dies behind them like the Maze has insulation in its bones.

Ut moves first, silent, careful.
Kiwi tracks seams and corners.
Axlot tests every step like he’s daring the floor to argue.

The corridor bends, and bends again. No sky visible now, only stone and vine and the feeling of being funneled.

They don’t see creatures. They don’t hear animals. The quiet is too deliberate.

Then Kiwi spots it.

Not a door. Not a panel. A lever.

A massive metal lever half-buried in a wall alcove, thick enough to take two hands, bolted like it’s meant to resist human strength.

Ut crouches, checks the ground around it. “No plate. No wire. Nothing obvious.”
Axlot flexes his hands. “Then we pull.”
Kiwi hesitates. “If it’s a trap, it’s a loud one.”

They decide anyway.

Axlot grabs the lever with both hands and hauls.

It doesn’t move.

He grits his teeth and pulls again, muscles trembling.

Ut steps in and adds weight to the motion, bracing his shoulder into the wall.

Kiwi grabs the handle too, hands stacked, and they pull together.

The lever gives with a brutal clang.

It slams down into place like a judge’s gavel.

Instantly an alarm blares, not in the Maze, but in Aurora’s Rest itself.

The sound hits the camp through stone like the walls are conducting it.

In the distance, a low hiss begins.

Back in Aurora’s Rest, the Peeps watch in disbelief as the invisible boundary line starts to “mist.”

A grey smog that had been sitting in empty space begins to thin, then peel away like fog being vacuumed.

A wall of it collapses silently.

And behind it, the world expands.

A hectare of dense forest appears as if it was always there and they were simply forbidden from seeing it.

Trees packed tight, tall and dark, trunks like pillars. Underbrush dense enough to hide a person. No birds. No insects. No animals at all.

Just forest.

space fan whispers, “It was right there.”
Lysrix answers, “It was always there.”
Duccarian adds, “And they let us earn it.”

-MAD$- exhales. “One quarter down. Three to go.”

Rally mally watches the new forest line, eyes hard. “We don’t rush it. First we get our people back.”

In the Maze, the alarm continues to pulse through the stone.

Ut, Kiwi, and Axlot turn back immediately, moving faster now.

The corridor seems the same, but everything feels sharper after the lever.

They’re almost at the threshold when Axlot steps on a slightly darker stone.

A pressure plate.

There’s a soft click.

Then a spring-loaded syringe snaps up from the floor and drives into his foot through the sole.

Axlot yelps and jerks back, swearing.

Kiwi’s face drains. “What was that.”
Ut is already down on one knee, grabbing Axlot’s ankle. “Don’t move.”
Axlot clenches his jaw, breathing hard. “It stabbed me.”

Ut checks the wound. A tiny puncture. No blood. Just a sting.

No one sees a creature.

No one hears anything else.

Kiwi whispers, “It injected something.”
Axlot tries to laugh it off. “So what. It’s a needle.”
Ut’s voice is flat. “No. It’s a trap. A dose.”

They don’t know what it is.

They only know it’s in him now.

They half-carry Axlot back through the threshold and into Aurora’s Rest.

Rally mally meets them immediately. “Report.”
Kiwi points back at the forest. “Lever. One zone unlocked.”
Ut says, “Maze has traps.”
Axlot lifts his foot and tries to wave it off. “Something stabbed me. I’m fine.”

Lysrix’s eyes narrow. “You’re not fine. Sit.”

space fan rushes to the supplies, grabbing cloth strips and water like she’s already becoming the person who fixes things.
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) stares at the puncture, swallowing. “That’s not normal.”
Nightmare watches Axlot with a faint, hungry interest. “So the Maze bites back.”

Rally mally looks at the forest, then at the elevator, then at the torn wall.

Then he says what nobody wants to hear.

“We don’t know what we just unlocked.”

“And we don’t know what that just put into him.”

“And night is coming.”

Audience note
The sting GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) takes tonight is from a special Griever trap designed to trigger his later transformation. The Peeps do not know this.

[[NIGHT]]
NIGHT 2 THE PALE STRIP

The light cools again without dimming.

Not sunset. Not night in a normal sense. Just a shift in tone, like the sky is a ceiling lamp and someone lowered the warmth to make everything feel less human.

Rally mally stands up the moment he feels it. “Same rules as last night. Pairs. No wandering. No one near the elevator.”

The elevator is still there in the ground, a square mouth cut into the grass, metal rails and a cage sunk low where Popcorn Rya and Elvvusz disappeared. They built a ring of stones around it during the day. Not for strength. For memory.

Ut checks the ring first, then the forest edge. “Stone line is intact.”
janhk. crouches by the seam where grass meets metal and listens with his palm flat. “It vibrates. Like cables settling.”
Kiwi watches the vines above the elevator mouth, eyes narrow. “It wants someone to forget. That’s how it wins.”
Duccarian answers quietly. “It wins either way. Forgetting just makes it faster.”

They use the last stretch of steady light to work, because work keeps fear from filling every gap.

-MAD$- sets ration packs on a cloth strip and counts them twice. “One packet each. No exceptions. Eat now or you get stupid later.”
space fan opens the little med supplies they have and lays them out like a ritual. Cloth. Water. A pen. Paper. “If someone gets cut, tell me. If someone starts shaking, tell me faster.”
Doomfox hovers near the tree line, restless and new. “So we just… build and wait.”
Ninnginni gives him a tired half smile. “Welcome to Aurora’s Rest. We do crafts until the world tries to kill us.”
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) helps anyway, quieter than earlier, pulling vines into bundles and handing them to Rally mally like he needs a job to stay real.
pinkfml sits under Dave the Tree with her knees pulled in, staring at the elevator mouth and not blinking for long stretches.
Nightmare chooses a watch position with his back near the boundary stones, facing inward. “If anyone panics, I see it first.”
Axlot paces. Too much. Too fast. Like the ground is irritating him.

Lysrix watches Axlot for a long time before she speaks. “You’re walking like you’re trying to shake something off.”
Axlot snaps back instantly. “I’m fine.”
Rally mally steps in, steady. “Sit. Drink. Stop burning energy.”
Axlot laughs once, sharp. “You’re not my dad.”
Nightmare mutters, almost amused. “He’s your leash, though.”
Axlot’s head whips toward Nightmare. “Say that again.”
Elvvusz would have softened it. There is no Elvvusz. The space where calm should be is empty.

Ut moves between them, voice flat. “Enough.”
janhk. adds without looking up. “The Maze likes noise.”

Axlot tries to sit. His foot twitches. He rubs his hands on his pants like something is crawling under the skin. The puncture from the syringe looks harmless, but his eyes don’t.

Kiwi keeps glancing at the forest edge, then at the elevator, then at the stone line. Like he’s reading a clock that only he can see.

Then the ground hum shifts.

The Maze begins to move.

Not a sudden slam. A long, grinding pressure, stone sliding against stone somewhere beyond their sight. The sound is lower tonight. Heavier. It lasts longer. It comes in waves, like the Maze is repositioning pieces slowly to make sure nobody can memorize it.

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) whispers, “It’s doing it again.”
Ninnginni’s joke voice is gone. “Yeah. It’s doing it on purpose.”
Doomfox swallows. “How long does it last.”
Duccarian answers like he already knows. “As long as it wants.”
Rally mally raises his voice just enough. “Everyone in shelters. Now.”

They huddle into the crude walls they built. Vines creak. Stones settle. The forest beyond the smog line makes a sound like branches scraping without wind.

Kiwi whispers to Lysrix, “Hear that.”
Lysrix nods once. “The Maze is changing the world while we can’t see it.”
space fan shifts closer to pinkfml. “Hey. Look at me. Breathe.”
pinkfml tries. Her breath comes out uneven anyway.

Axlot jolts up suddenly like he heard his name. “Someone is watching me.”
Rally mally keeps his voice even. “We are all watching each other. That’s the point.”
Axlot points at the elevator. “It moved. It moved when nobody touched it.”
janhk. answers quietly, still listening to the ground. “Cables settle when the Maze shifts. Doesn’t mean it rose.”
Axlot steps toward the stone ring anyway.
Ut blocks him without aggression, just body. “No.”

Axlot’s face twists, anger too fast. “Move.”
Ut doesn’t. “No.”
Nightmare smiles faintly in the dim. “He’s going to swing.”
Axlot’s fist tightens, then loosens. He shakes like he’s holding himself down by force. He spits to the side and backs away, breathing hard.

Rally mally watches him, then makes a decision. “Axlot stays with me tonight. No debate.”
Axlot snarls. “I don’t need a babysitter.”
Rally mally answers, voice flat. “You do.”

And while everyone is focused on the shifting stone and the tension inside the shelters, curiosity finds a gap.

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) slips away.

Not far. Not dramatic. Just a few steps toward the elevator zone, drawn by the wrongness of it. The ground there looks different even in dim light.

Right next to the elevator mouth is a strip of grass that does not match the rest of Aurora’s Rest. The blades are shorter and pale, packed too tight, laying flat like wet velvet. There is no spring to it, no give. It feels cold through a shoe, like standing on metal with a skin over it. The air above it is heavier, like invisible smog still clings there even when you cannot see it.

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) looks back once and sees nobody watching.

He steps onto the pale strip.

A tiny click answers him.

He flinches, looks down, and feels a sharp sting in his ankle, fast as a thorn. He yelps under his breath and stumbles back off the strip immediately, heart hammering.

He checks the spot with his fingers. A pinprick. Almost nothing. No blood. No pain after the first second.

He hides it.

Because shame is quick, and fear is quicker.

He returns to the shelter line with the same bundle of vines he left with, like nothing happened.

Rally mally is too busy keeping Axlot from boiling over.
space fan is too busy calming pinkfml.
Ut and janhk. are too busy listening to the Maze grind.
Kiwi is too busy watching the elevator mouth.
Lysrix is too busy watching faces.

Only Nightmare notices GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) came back different.

Nightmare says nothing.

The Maze shifting slows. The grinding fades. The world stops moving.

Silence drops so hard it feels like a new wall.

For a few minutes, it almost feels safe.

Then GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) starts shaking.

At first it looks like cold. He hugs himself, jaw clenched. Then his muscles lock wrong. His breath catches. His knees buckle.

space fan is there instantly. “GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast). Hey. Look at me.”
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) tries to speak and only air comes out.
Lysrix drops beside space fan, voice sharp but controlled. “Hold his head. Keep him from biting his tongue.”
Rally mally looks over and his face goes tight. “What happened.”
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) can’t answer. His body jerks again.

Kiwi watches the pale strip near the elevator, then GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast), then the strip again. Like the pattern just revealed itself but he can’t prove it.

Ut kneels on the other side, steady hands. “Don’t restrain hard. Just keep him from hitting the ground.”
janhk. whispers, almost to himself. “A delayed trigger.”
Duccarian watches the convulsions like he’s trying to measure duration. “The Maze collects behavior. This is behavior.”
Ninnginni’s voice cracks. “Stop talking like it’s normal.”

pinkfml sits frozen under Dave the Tree, eyes wide, watching the shaking body like it might be her next.
Doomfox stands helpless, clutching a vine bundle like a tool he doesn’t understand. “Is he dying.”
Rally mally answers without looking away. “Not if we do this right.”

Nightmare stays on the edge of the group, arms crossed, expression unreadable. He looks almost satisfied that something finally happened to break the quiet.

The convulsions last long enough that everyone’s legs start cramping from holding positions. Long enough that time stops feeling measurable again. Long enough that the forest feels closer even though nobody moved.

Then, slowly, GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) stops shaking.

He goes limp, breathing hard, sweat cold on his skin.

space fan exhales like she forgot she could. “Okay. Okay. Stay with me.”
Lysrix wipes hair out of her face and speaks softly now. “You’re still here.”
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) blinks and nods once, weak but present.

Rally mally looks at the group, voice low. “We give him a chance. We don’t turn into the Maze.”
Ut nods. “We watch him.”
janhk. nods too. “We document everything.”
Kiwi keeps his eyes on the elevator mouth. “We also watch that.”

Axlot laughs under his breath, bitter. “Everyone’s getting sick now. Great.”
Rally mally meets his eyes. “You’re next if you keep pushing.”
Axlot’s smile dies. He sits. Finally.

The shelters hold.

Barely.

Nobody sleeps.

They just listen to the silence and wait for the next grind.

Because now they know Night 2 does not only bring moving walls.

It brings the Maze inside their bodies too.
