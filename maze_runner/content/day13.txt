[[DAY]]
DAY 13 — THE DOOR AT THE END OF THE WORLD

The morning does not begin.
It resumes.

Ash still settles in places where no wind moves. The waterfall still runs grey. The markers still stand. The Maze doors still wait.

Rally mally stands in the center of the ruins with the runner notes folded once in his hand.

“Everyone goes in,” he says.
“Except space fan.”

No one questions the order. Only the reason.

“You are base,” he tells her. “If something breaches while we’re inside, you’re the line.”

space fan nods once.  
“Then come back with everyone.”

“That’s the plan.”

Golden adjusts his grip on a salvaged tool.  
“Rear anchor confirmed.”

Polaris rolls her shoulders once.  
“Recon takes point. Hand signals only.”

Toad exhales.  
“Well. That’s not terrifying at all.”

SomeDucks says quietly,  
“Today we stop reacting. Today we start reading.”

Galaxy is already at the doors.

Duccarian crouches, placing Frodge beside space fan.  
“Guard the idea,” he tells the duck softly.

Trxty points to the hinge housing.  
“I need twenty seconds.”

“You have fifteen,” Lysrix replies.

JIKOSU is already inside.

Rabbit follows.

They enter the Maze.

---

The corridors feel slower.

Not physically.
Relationally.

The Maze has updated its expectations of them.  
They have updated theirs of it.

Polaris and Rabbit move ahead.  
Rally mally and JIKOSU follow with maps.  
Lysrix watches flanks.  
Golden anchors rear.  
Toad carries supply.  
Duccarian and SomeDucks move in silent parallel.  
Trxty scans junctions.  
Galaxy moves outside formation entirely.

The Quigg screams are distant.

Which means they are elsewhere.

---

Rabbit misses a turn.

Only briefly.

The corridor he finds is narrow, darker, unused.

Something moves.

He crouches.

A duck steps out of the darkness.

Not Frodge.

Darker. Sharper. Quieter.

It studies him with perfect calm.

“Hello,” Rabbit says.

The duck steps onto his knee.

Decision made.

When Polaris finds him, she simply says,  
“We’re moving.”

He nods.

The duck stays with him.

---

They find the nest.

The chamber opens like an industrial cathedral.

Mechanical growth.
Biological production.
Pods.
Pits.
Development.

Quiggs are not born.

They are assembled.

Golden whispers,  
“They’re inventory.”

SomeDucks says,  
“War always is.”

Toad mutters,  
“I hate this room.”

Trxty observes the shell structures.  
“Integration ratio controlled. Designed evolution.”

Rally mally says,  
“We leave it intact.”

Galaxy holds the entrance.

They move on.

---

The corridor runs straight.

Too straight.

Then it ends.

At a door.

Smooth metal.  
Seven symbol plates.  
Six lock columns.  
Indicator lights alive.

Polaris breathes once.  
“That’s real.”

Golden studies the panels.  
“System override. Not mechanical.”

JIKOSU says quietly,  
“These correspond to Maze nodes.”

Lysrix turns.  
“You’ve seen them.”

“Yes.”

Silence.

Rally mally says,  
“We log everything. We come back prepared.”

SomeDucks studies the door.  
“The Maze planned an ending. The question is who earns it.”

Trxty memorizes the symbols.

Rabbit memorizes the sequence.

Then the air changes.

Cooling.

“Move,” Rally mally says.

---

The return fractures.

Walls shift early.

Toad is separated.  
Golden finds the parallel route.  
Reintegration occurs with seconds to spare.

They run the final corridor.

Galaxy is already outside.

They all make it.

The doors slam.

---

space fan checks them.

Then she sees the duck.

Then Frodge sees the duck.

Recognition passes without ceremony.

They stand together.

Then separate.

Duccarian studies the new one.  
“Frucket.”

Rabbit blinks.  
“What?”

Duccarian says with a calm tone.
“That’s the name.”

Rabbit nods slowly.  
“…Frucket.”

The duck accepts.

SomeDucks says,  
“Frodge is memory. Frucket is motion.”

Toad sighs.  
“They’re ducks.”

Duccarian replies,  
“Nothing here is just what it looks like.”

---

They have a target now.

A real one.

A door.

Seven symbols.

And JIKOSU finally says,

“I know where two nodes are.”

Rally mally looks at him.

“Then tonight you tell me everything.”

JIKOSU nods.

Agreement reached.

The first real one.

[[NARRATIVE]]

[[NIGHT]]
NIGHT 13 — THE SILENCE THAT SHOULD NOT EXIST

The Maze is quiet.

Not calm. Not resting. Quiet in the way a machine is quiet when it has powered down for maintenance — the specific absence that belongs not to peace, but to preparation. A held breath before a system resets into a configuration that has not existed before.

No Quigg screams. No wall shifts. No distant grinding. No subsonic hum rising through the ground into the chest.

Aurora’s Rest — ash, markers, two ducks, and the flat stone covered in symbol sketches — exists tonight in suspension. The system that has been reacting to them for thirteen days has paused. Or stopped reacting. Or shifted into a mode where reaction is no longer the operative word.

Rally mally sits beside the flat stone, forearms resting on his knees, eyes fixed on the Maze doors.

“They are watching,” he says.

JIKOSU, beside him, shakes his head slowly. It is not contradiction. It is correction.

“Tonight they are calculating.”

The silence absorbs both statements and offers nothing back.

Trxty kneels at the stone and begins the symbol work without ceremony. Ceremony takes time. The symbols have been waiting since the moment they found the door, and time is the Maze’s most controlled resource.

He draws the first symbol from memory — the vertical line broken by two cross-fractures.

“Structural marker,” he says. “Foundation node. Something load-bearing.”

Polaris leans over the stone.

“Not fracture. Interruption. The lines disrupt continuity. Something stopped mid-flow.”

Golden studies both interpretations.

“It’s a lock. Everything in this place is a lock wearing different language.”

Rabbit studies the second symbol — the spiral that almost closes.

“Not spiral. Loop,” he says quietly. “Something designed to return, forced open before completion.”

SomeDucks nods.

“Escape begins when repetition fails.”

Toad sighs.

“Can we describe the murder-machine door without philosophy for thirty seconds?”

Duccarian replies without looking up.

“Meaning is how we carry what facts alone would break.”

Toad exhales and keeps watching.

Lysrix traces the third symbol in the air above the drawing.

“Three triangles joined at their tips. Convergence. A meeting point.”

space fan writes each interpretation on salvaged paper in her precise clinical hand.

“If we don’t make it out,” she says, “someone else will need this legible.”

No one argues. No one thanks her. It is simply true.

Galaxy does not sit. He circles the perimeter of the ruins, slow and territorial. Each pass, his gaze crosses the stone, the faces, the doors. Stillness tonight feels like vulnerability.

Then JIKOSU begins.

Not a lecture. Not a confession. A controlled release.

“The Maze is not a puzzle. It is testing architecture. Corridors are variables. Nodes regulate behavioral response zones. Wall shifts are calibration, not reconfiguration. The Maze adapts based on what it records.”

Rally mally watches him with full attention.

“You worked here.”

A weighted pause.

“Not willingly,” JIKOSU says. “But yes.”

Silence rearranges the room without touching anything.

Golden stands.

Not dramatically. Structurally.

“You knew.”

“I knew fragments,” JIKOSU says. “Memory returns under pressure. Being here restored more. But I did not know about the door.”

Golden turns toward the Maze doors.

“I’m done waiting for it to teach us how to survive. When we find the override, we fight.”

Polaris watches him.

“He’s deteriorating,” she murmurs to Rabbit.

Rabbit shakes his head.

“No. He’s accelerating.”

She considers this. Files it.

They build the map.

Not a runner’s map.

A war map.

Symbol nodes. Patrol timing. Wall memory. Acoustic anomalies. Ventilation vectors preserved from Kiwi’s notes.

Lines intersect. Hypotheses break. Corrections form.

They are no longer reacting.

They are modeling.

SomeDucks steps back.

“We are no longer prey.”

Duccarian adds,

“We are becoming authors.”

Toad rubs his face.

“I preferred farming.”

Near the dying fire, Frodge and Frucket sit together. Close. Not touching. A relationship expressed in proximity.

Rabbit watches them.

“They already know how to survive.”

Polaris replies,

“Animals don’t overthink survival.”

“Maybe we shouldn’t either.”

They sit closer than before. Not touching. Aligned.

Lysrix notices. Says nothing.  
space fan notices. Approves silently.  
Golden notices. Looks back to the Maze doors.

The silence continues.

Hours pass like resistance in water.

Trxty checks the doors repeatedly.

“This duration is statistically impossible,” he says.

JIKOSU answers,

“They are preparing a phase change.”

The temperature of the moment drops.

Galaxy completes his circuit. Stops. Looks at each of them once.

Then he sits.

The meaning is immediate.

If the predator rests, escalation follows.

They sleep.

Not deeply. Not fully. But truly.

Rally mally sleeps with his hand resting on the notes.  
JIKOSU sleeps upright.  
Polaris sleeps lightly.  
Rabbit sleeps beside Frucket.  
space fan sleeps last.  
Golden sleeps briefly, then remains awake.  
Galaxy sleeps like a cooling weapon.  
SomeDucks dreams in pattern.  
Duccarian dreams in memory.  
Toad dreams of ordinary things.  
Lysrix dreams without fear.  
Trxty does not dream.

The Maze does not move.

The night ends without event.

Which is the most unnatural event yet.

Because every other night gave them something.

This one gives them nothing.

Which means the Maze chose silence.

Which means it is saving something.

Because when war gives you a night,

you take it.
