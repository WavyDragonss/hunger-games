[[DAY]]
DAY 3 — THE SECOND LEVER

[[NARRATIVE]]
Day 3 arrives without ceremony.

The light warms again like nothing happened, like the Maze didn’t grind all night and like GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) didn’t convulse in the dirt while everyone tried not to panic.

Nobody feels rested. They just feel awake.

Rally mally does headcount first. He forces the words out like a ritual. “Say your name when I call it.”

Axlot answers too fast, like he’s daring someone to accuse him.
Nightmare answers without looking up.
Duccarian answers calmly.
-MAD$- answers while already sorting ration scraps.
space fan answers while checking GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast)’s face for fever.
Lysrix answers quietly, eyes flicking to the elevator zone.
Kiwi answers and keeps his gaze away from the pale strip.
Ut answers and steps toward the boundary stones immediately.
janhk. answers and starts writing notches into the soil again.
Ninnginni answers with a weak joke voice that doesn’t fully return.
pinkfml answers in a whisper, still pale.
Doomfox answers like he’s surprised he’s still here.
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) answers last, voice hoarse, and then looks down at his ankle like it betrayed him.

Rally mally makes the first rule of the day. “No one approaches the elevator zone alone. Not even for a second.”

Axlot opens his mouth.
Lysrix beats him to it. “Agreed.”
Kiwi nods once, too quickly.
Nightmare smiles faintly like agreement is a tool he can use later.

They eat the last of the nutrient bars. Not enough. Never enough.

-MAD$- hands out portions without argument. “We’re not collapsing today. Eat.”
space fan keeps half a water packet aside and holds it to GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast)’s lips. “Small sips. Don’t gulp.”
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) tries to joke and fails. “Dave the Tree is judging me.”
Ninnginni gives him a real smile this time. “Dave judges all of us.”

The forest hectare they unlocked yesterday sits there, dense and silent, still no animals, still no life noises. It looks like a picture someone painted and forgot to finish.

Ut comes back from the tree line with two handfuls of dry branches and a report. “No tracks. No droppings. No insects. That’s unnatural.”
janhk. adds from the seam line, “It’s a constructed biome. It’s there to be harvested.”
Duccarian murmurs, “Or to hide things.”
Kiwi says, “Or both.”

Rally mally doesn’t let them spiral. “Today we unlock another zone. We need more space. More material. And we need to know where the next lever is.”

Nightmare steps forward like he’s been waiting for permission. “Then we go.”
Rally mally shakes his head. “Three people. Fast. Quiet. Smart.”
Axlot laughs. “You mean not me.”
Rally mally meets his eyes. “Not you.”

Axlot’s jaw tightens, but he doesn’t argue this time. He just looks away like he’s holding a grudge in his teeth.

Rally mally points to the team. “Ut. Kiwi. janhk.”
janhk. pauses mid-scratch. “Me.”
Rally mally answers, “Yes. You read seams. You spot tracks. You’re going.”
Kiwi swallows. “Okay.”
Ut nods once. “In and out.”

space fan steps forward. “If you find anything that stings, snaps, or clicks, you don’t touch it. You come back.”
Lysrix adds quietly, “And if the lights change, you freeze.”
Duccarian says, “If the Maze wants you dead, it won’t announce it.”
Ninnginni mutters, “Great pep talk.”

They go.

The Maze entrance is open wider today, like it expects them now. Like the system rewarded yesterday’s lever pull with a deeper corridor.

The air inside is colder than Aurora’s Rest. Sound gets swallowed. Even footsteps feel wrong, like the floor is damp stone pretending to be dry.

Ut leads. Kiwi watches corners. janhk. watches seams.

They move slowly at first, then faster as confidence returns.

They pass one pressure plate that Ut spots in time. He points, and Kiwi steps around it without speaking.

janhk. crouches, studies the mechanism. “Spring housing. Needle slot. Similar to last one.”
Kiwi’s throat tightens. “So there are more.”
Ut says, “We treat every stone like it wants blood.”

They follow the corridor deeper than yesterday, guided by nothing except instinct and the idea that the lever has to exist somewhere.

Then they find it.

A larger alcove, a metal handle bolted into stone, thick enough to require weight and will.

Ut checks the floor first. Nothing obvious.

Kiwi checks the wall edges. No seam traps.

janhk. checks the lever mount itself. “It’s reinforced. It expects force.”
Ut braces. “On three.”
Kiwi places his hands too.
janhk. stacks in.

They pull.

The lever doesn’t move.

They pull again.

It gives with a brutal clang and slams into place like the Maze just accepted a payment.

An alarm detonates through the walls.

Not a siren. A clean tone, like a system confirmation.

Back in Aurora’s Rest, the Peeps freeze at the sound.

Nightmare’s head snaps up. “That’s the lever.”
Rally mally says, “Hold positions.”
Lysrix watches the invisible boundary line like it might peel away.

It does.

The smog barrier on another side of Aurora’s Rest thins, then collapses like fog being vacuumed.

A new hectare reveals itself.

Not forest this time.

A grid of orchard-like trees in perfect rows, spaced too evenly, trunks identical thickness, branches pruned into the same shapes. Between rows, the ground is bare dirt with faint lines—like it was already meant for planting.

Doomfox whispers, “That’s… farmland.”
-MAD$- breathes out hard. “That’s survival.”
space fan says, “That’s hope.”
Duccarian murmurs, “That’s expectation.”
Lysrix says quietly, “That’s a cage that gets bigger.”

The Maze team returns.

Ut comes in first, hand up, signaling no chase.
Kiwi follows, eyes wide.
janhk. comes last, breathing hard, but smiling for the first time since waking up.

Rally mally meets them at the boundary. “Report.”
Ut answers, “Lever two. More traps deeper in.”
Kiwi adds, “Pressure plates. Needle housings. We can spot them if we slow down.”
janhk. says, “The Maze is designed. That means we can learn it.”

Rally mally nods. “Good. We expand camp toward the orchard zone. We build better shelter. We plant.”
-MAD$- is already moving. “Axes to the forest. Hoe to the orchard. Seeds get inventoried.”
Ninnginni exhales. “We’re farming in a nightmare. Cool.”

GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) stands up too fast and sways. space fan catches him.
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) mutters, “I’m fine.”
Lysrix watches him carefully. “No you’re not.”
Kiwi looks at GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast)’s ankle and then looks away like the thought hurts.

Axlot watches the lever team with narrowed eyes, anger mixing with something like fear. “So they trust you now.”
Ut doesn’t respond.
janhk. doesn’t respond.
Kiwi doesn’t respond.

Rally mally does. “We trust competence. Earn it.”
Axlot’s face tightens. “I did earn it.”
Rally mally’s voice is flat. “Not lately.”

Nightmare laughs once, quiet. “Good. The group fractures. That’s always fun.”

Duccarian looks toward the Maze corridor, calm as ever. “Two zones unlocked. Two remain.”
Lysrix answers, “And the Maze already took payment.”
space fan whispers, looking at the orchard rows, “We’re growing food under a sky that isn’t real.”

Rally mally raises his voice just enough to keep everyone in one story. “We build. We plant. We rest when we can. And tonight we watch GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast). And we watch Axlot.”

No one argues.

Because the day feels like progress.

And progress in this place always means the system is preparing something else.
