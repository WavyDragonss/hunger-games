[[DAY]]
DAY 7 — THE VILLAGE AND THE WATER

[[NARRATIVE]]
Day 7 arrives like it does not know what happened.

The waterfall sound fills Aurora's Rest before anyone opens their eyes. It is constant and smooth and strangely perfect, a sheet of water falling into a dark basin that should overflow but never does. The Peeps do not know it recirculates. To them it looks like the world has a hole in it and something decided to pour forever into the gap.

It is the first morning that does not start in silence.

Rally mally is already standing.

He calls headcount before the camp finishes waking, the same voice he always uses, flat and present, like certainty is a posture he chose and has not put down since.

Kiwi answers and sits up straight like he was not asleep.
Lysrix answers and marks who looks functional and who is running on the wrong kind of fuel.
Ut answers and pulls out his map cloth before Rally finishes saying his name.
janhk. answers and checks the stone seam near his sleeping spot, old habit, reading the surface.
Ninnginni answers with nothing added. No joke. Not this morning.
space fan answers while counting the medical supply strips by touch.
Toad answers while already moving containers, already working.
Duccarian answers quietly, sitting near the waterfall zone edge, eyes calm.
Nightmare answers without looking at Rally, staring at the Maze doors like they owe him something.
Cyrus answers and rubs both hands over his face hard.
SomeDucks answers slowly, watching the waterfall from a distance like he is cataloguing the sound.
Toad answers and immediately checks the fermentation pot, lifting the cloth seal an inch and inhaling with professional focus.
pinkfml does not answer.

Rally mally waits one beat. Then two. "pinkfml."

She is already awake. Already sitting. Already staring at the Maze doors with the particular blankness of someone who spent the night somewhere they cannot describe.

She answers her own name like she is confirming something she is not sure of.

Rally mally moves on.

"GalaxyTwentea."

From the cage, a claw taps the bar once. A forearm drops to the dirt and scratches a mark. Present.

Rally mally looks at the mark. Then at the cage. Then at the group.

He speaks before anyone can start the morning work.

"I want to say something about Galaxy."

The camp pays attention the way tired people do, bodies still, eyes awake.

"He has worked. He has listened. He has not hurt anyone. He warned us in Night 6 before some of us were even watching." He pauses. "We built that cage because we were afraid. I am still afraid. But I think we are keeping him locked because it is easy, not because it is right."

Ut says immediately, "Rules first."
space fan says, "He should be free."
Kiwi says, "Daytime only. He earns the nights."
janhk. says, "If he moves toward the Maze we cannot stop him."
Nightmare says, "He is not a person."
SomeDucks says quietly, "He remembers being one."

Rally mally lets the responses settle. "Galaxy is free during daylight hours. He sleeps in a structure we build for him, not the cage. He stays inside Aurora's Rest. He does not approach the Maze doors without a runner team present."

He walks to the cage latch himself and opens it.

Galaxy does not burst out.

He steps out slowly, unfolding to full height, blinking in the open light. He is enormous and wrong-shaped and somehow careful, the way something strong can choose to take up less space than it owns. He stands in the morning air and turns his head once around the full perimeter of Aurora's Rest like he is measuring it.

Then he scratches in the dirt.

THANK YOU.

space fan exhales like she has been holding it since Night 2.
Toad says, "Well. Good morning then."
Ninnginni grins despite himself. "He has better manners than Nightmare."
Nightmare says flatly, "I am not competing with a lizard."
Kiwi watches Galaxy for a long moment, then looks away, something uncoiling in his shoulders.

---

The morning builds into the busiest day Aurora's Rest has seen.

Rally mally organizes three streams of work running at once.

Ut and janhk. plan the water transport system, sketching shallow channel lines and testing container weight from the waterfall basin to the central camp. They argue quietly about gradient and stone composition like engineers who forgot they were trapped.

Cyrus and Toad work the orchard farmland, turning soil rows and planting seeds from the supply crates. Toad counts seeds like currency. Cyrus talks to himself about root depth, referencing a sun that is not real but behaves like one.

Toad tends the fermentation pot with the patience of someone who has decided patience is the one skill Aurora's Rest cannot teach.

"Four more days. Maybe three if the warmth holds." He checks the cloth seal. "When it is done it will not taste like anything good. It will taste like we planned to be here long enough to make it."

Ninnginni leans over his shoulder. "What does that taste like."

Toad replaces the seal carefully. "Like a reason."

---

And then there is Galaxy building his own hut.

Rally mally assigns Cyrus to design it and Galaxy to build it, and the combination is immediately something worth watching.

Cyrus sketches a low structure in the dirt, slightly away from the main sleeping area, sturdier than the others, wider at the base, roof line sloping against the direction the Maze sounds come from. He explains the reasoning with hand gestures and drawn lines.

Galaxy watches, then picks up a log that would take three Peeps to carry and moves it to the marked spot with the particular casualness of something that has recently discovered it is very strong.

Cyrus stares.

Galaxy scratches in the dirt.

WHERE.

Cyrus points. Galaxy places the log precisely. He does not drop it. He sets it down.

"He is better at this than half the crew," Cyrus mutters.

janhk. walks past carrying rope. "Do not let Nightmare hear you say that."

By midmorning the frame is up. By midday the walls are taking shape. Galaxy works with methodical focus, pausing only to scratch practical notes. CORNER WEAK. THIS SIDE. MORE HERE. The hut becomes real faster than anything they have built, because the builder does not get tired the normal way and does not second-guess the weight of things.

When the walls are standing, Galaxy steps inside, turns around once, and lies down to test the space. He fits, barely, but intentionally. He has built it to his exact dimensions.

He scratches from inside it.

GOOD.

SomeDucks, watching from the treeline, says quietly, "He remembers what home means."

Duccarian, sitting nearby with his back against a tree, says nothing, but watches Galaxy's face through the hut doorway with an expression that is working something through.

---

The Duccarian and Frodge moment comes quietly, the way important things do.

The duck had been moving through the waterfall zone on its own logic all morning, stepping between wet stones like the moss and mist were built for small things. It had ignored every Peep who tried to approach it. Ninnginni got closest and the duck walked away from him with what looked like judgment.

When Duccarian comes to sit by the waterfall basin, he does not try to approach it.

He just sits.

Frodge notices him. Walks toward him. Steps directly up to his boot and looks up at him.

Duccarian looks down.

He does not reach for the duck. He just waits.

Frodge climbs onto his knee.

SomeDucks, sitting nearby, says softly, "He does not do that."

Duccarian looks at the duck sitting on his knee, feathers damp from the basin mist, small eyes steady and unafraid.

"This is the only living thing in Aurora's Rest that does not know it should be terrified," he says.

SomeDucks thinks about that. "Maybe that is what we need. One thing that does not know."

Duccarian lifts Frodge gently, turning the duck to look at him properly. Frodge endures this without complaint.

"The Maze made all of this," Duccarian says, still looking at the duck. "The walls. The traps. The lever that punished Doomfox for doing exactly what it asked him to do." He pauses. "And then it put a duck in the waterfall."

SomeDucks watches him. "You think that was a mistake."

Duccarian shakes his head. "I think the Maze planned every single thing except this. The duck found a crack in the design. Something small enough to slip through unnoticed."

Frodge makes a small sound.

Duccarian says, "Exactly."

From the camp, Ninnginni spots Duccarian sitting with a duck on his knee, speaking to it philosophically while the waterfall falls behind him, and he nudges Toad and points.

Toad watches for a moment. "Is he having a conversation with the duck."

Ninnginni whispers, "I think the duck is winning."

Kiwi hears them and looks over, and for a brief second he almost looks like a normal person.

pinkfml hears the laughter from where she is sitting against the orchard fence line.

She looks up.

She watches Duccarian holding the duck. She watches Kiwi's face do something close to ease.

She almost smiles.

Then she looks back at the Maze doors and the almost-smile dissolves like it was borrowed.

---

[[NARRATIVE]]
pinkfml's day has a different texture from everyone else's.

It does not announce itself. It is the texture of something quietly coming loose.

She does her morning tasks, carrying water, stacking supply cloth, sitting where she is supposed to sit. But she keeps losing the thread of each task halfway through. She sets a container down and forgets she was carrying it. She stands in the middle of the orchard path for a few seconds too long before remembering which direction she was going.

space fan notices. She does not say anything direct. She just starts positioning herself closer to pinkfml's orbit, appearing near her at regular intervals, handing her things to do that are simple and require full hands.

Lysrix notices and says nothing at all. She watches from a distance with eyes that are counting something.

At one point pinkfml asks space fan a question in a flat, careful voice.

"Did he scream for long."

space fan does not pretend not to understand. "pinkfml—"

"Doomfox." Still flat. Still careful. "When the Quigg took him. Did it last long."

space fan holds her gaze. "No."

pinkfml nods once, like confirming data. She goes back to stacking supply cloth.

Later, near the orchard fence, pinkfml looks at her hands for a long time. They are clean. She has washed them. But she turns them over and over like there is something on them that normal washing cannot reach.

Duccarian passes and sees this.

He does not stop. But he looks at her hands and then at her face, and something shifts in his expression that is not quite pity and not quite recognition but lives somewhere between them.

He does not say anything.

He is not sure yet that words would reach where she is.

---

The elevator rises mid-afternoon.

The camp steps back automatically, giving it space the way they always do now.

A new Peep steps out.

Rabbit.

He is fast-looking even standing still, the kind of person whose stillness reads like stored speed. He takes in the camp in one long sweep, the hut, the waterfall, the cage sitting open and empty, the raptor carrying logs in the background, and he exhales through his nose like he is deciding whether to be afraid.

He looks at Rally mally. "You are in charge."

It is not a question.

Rally mally nods. "Name."

"Rabbit."

"You follow rules and you eat. Then you help."

Rabbit glances at Galaxy moving across the camp with a beam under one arm. "Is that—"

"One of us," Rally mally says. "He cannot speak. He understands everything. Treat him accordingly."

Rabbit absorbs this, nods once, and steps fully into Aurora's Rest.

Ninnginni appears at his shoulder immediately. "Welcome to the place that is trying to kill us elegantly. I am Ninnginni. We have beer almost ready. We have a duck. It is a lot to take in."

Rabbit stares at him.

Ninnginni continues, "The duck is named Frodge. You will understand when you meet him."

---

Then the light begins to cool.

Not gradually. A shift, the way a mood changes the temperature of a room.

Rally mally calls everyone in from the outer zones.

But pinkfml is at the waterfall edge again.

space fan moves toward the waterfall zone first. "pinkfml. Time to come in."

pinkfml is crouched at the basin rim, shoes on, hands in the water. She does not answer immediately.

space fan reaches her and crouches beside her. "We have to go in now."

pinkfml watches the water move over her fingers. "I know."

But she does not move.

She says, soft and flat, "I am so tired of being scared."

space fan rests a hand on her back. "Come on. Inside. Together."

pinkfml starts to stand.

Her foot slips.

It is not a movie slip. There is no suspended moment. One foot goes sideways on the moss and she drops fast, hip catching the basin rim, head striking the stone ledge with a sound that is too quiet for what it means.

She slides into the shallow edge of the basin and does not move.

The waterfall continues.

Smooth. Endless. Indifferent.

space fan's scream brings the camp.

---

Rally mally reaches the waterfall zone at a run with Lysrix beside him.

space fan is already in the shallows, hands on pinkfml, doing everything she knows how to do in the order she knows to do it.

She sits back.

She shakes her head once.

The waterfall continues.

---

[[NARRATIVE]]
The camp does not process it immediately.

Rally mally stands and looks at pinkfml's face and does not speak. When he finally turns, his expression is not grief yet. It is the face of someone holding something in both hands that he does not know how to put down.

space fan stays kneeling, hands still, not moving away.

Kiwi walks to the orchard fence and grips the wood and stares at nothing and does not turn around for a while.

Ut sits down where he is standing and pulls out his map cloth and stares at it without marking anything.

janhk. goes to the basin edge and examines the stone. He studies the moss, the slope, the angle of the ledge with the same focused attention he gives to pressure plates and seam lines. He is looking for the mechanism. He cannot find one. That is somehow the worst part.

Ninnginni sits against a tree and puts his face in his hands.

Toad sets down the supply container he was carrying very carefully and does not pick it up again.

Cyrus says, quietly, to no one, "No."

SomeDucks stands near the waterfall and watches the water fall and says nothing for a while. Then he says, almost to himself, "She was trying to clean her hands."

Nightmare stands apart and looks at pinkfml's stillness. He says, in his flat honest way, "The Maze did not even need to touch her. It just needed to take enough from her that she stopped being careful." He does not say it cruelly. He says it like a fact he wishes was wrong.

Nobody answers him. Because nobody can argue with it.

Rabbit stands at the edge of the group, ten minutes in Aurora's Rest, holding the weight of something he was not warned about. He says nothing. He does not know her name yet. He learns it from the way everyone else says nothing.

GalaxyTwentea approaches slowly. The others make room without being asked. Galaxy stands over pinkfml for a moment, head low, and the mechanical quality of his raptor body seems to soften somehow, or maybe the group's perception of it does.

She crouches and scratches in the dirt beside pinkfml's hand.

SORRY.

Toad looks at his fermentation pot.

He looks at it for a long time.

He walks to it. Lifts the cloth seal. Checks the progress.

Replaces the seal.

He marks something small in the dirt beside the pot. A private notation.

Duccarian holds Frodge against his chest, one hand pressed firm over the duck's back. He speaks the way he sometimes does, like the thought has been waiting and the silence finally gave it room.

"Aurora's Rest is becoming a village," he says. "The hut. The water channels. The orchard rows. The beer fermenting in the pot." He pauses. "Villages bury people. That is not a failure of the village. That is what villages are. Places where lives happen. Including the ends of them."

SomeDucks says, soft and precise, "The Maze built a cage. We built a home inside it. It did not plan for that."

Duccarian nods. "So it reminded us of the walls."

---

They bury pinkfml in the orchard.

Near the edge, where the grass meets the first row of planted seeds, where the soil is soft and the waterfall sound reaches. Rally mally decides this without asking for opinions and nobody offers them.

They use their hands and the flat edge of a digging tool. The work is quiet. Galaxy carries the stones for the marker, choosing them from the waterfall zone with what looks like deliberate attention to size and shape. Nobody tells her to do this.

Duccarian holds Frodge through all of it.

Frodge does not struggle. Does not make noise. As if proximity to Duccarian is enough to make strange things feel less strange.

When it is done, Lysrix speaks simply, without ceremony. "She was here. She tried. The Maze was harder than that was enough."

space fan keeps her eyes on the orchard rows.

Kiwi crouches and touches the soil once, then stands.

Rabbit stands at the back of the group with his hands at his sides, watching the people he has just met bury someone, learning everything about Aurora's Rest from this one afternoon.

Toad is the last to move away.

He walks back to the fermentation pot. He checks the progress. He replaces the seal.

SomeDucks asks, voice still rough, "What did you mark in the dirt."

Toad exhales. "A note to myself."

He pauses.

"The beer will finish in three days. I wanted to remember who it was supposed to be for."

SomeDucks looks at the pot.

He sits down beside it. Close enough to hear if it makes a sound.

---

The light cools.

The Maze doors start their grinding cycle as though nothing happened.

Rally mally calls everyone in.

They come. All of them.

The count is smaller than it was on Day 1 and they all feel the arithmetic of it differently.

As the camp settles into night formation, Duccarian finds a place near the shelter line with Frodge still against his chest. The duck shifts once, settling deeper into the warmth of him, and Duccarian presses his hand over its small back.

The waterfall falls in the dark.

The Maze shifts.

The Quigg screams begin their night circuit far away in the stone corridors.

And Aurora's Rest holds its shape. Water in the basin. Seeds in the soil. Beer breathing in the pot. A hut built by something that used to be human. A grave in the orchard with waterfall stones at its head.

A village.

A cage.

Both at once.

The Maze does not care which word they use.

[[NIGHT]]
NIGHT 7 — THE MESSAGE BEFORE THE STORM

The burial changes the quality of the dark.

It is the same dark as every other night in Aurora's Rest, the same artificial ceiling, the same 90 meter walls holding the black in place. But the camp feels it differently. Like the orchard soil is still warm from the day's work and the warmth is the wrong kind.

The waterfall runs.

It has not stopped once since Day 6 and it will not stop tonight. The sound fills the silence between Quigg screams, smooth and constant and completely indifferent to what was buried beneath the orchard trees this afternoon.

Rally mally sits against the shelter line with his back straight and his eyes half open. Not asleep. Not fully present either. Somewhere in the narrow corridor between the two where leaders go when they cannot afford either option.

Lysrix does not sleep. She watches the camp the way she always watches it, cataloguing breathing patterns, tracking who shifts and who lies too still. space fan is finally unconscious, exhausted past the point where grief can keep her awake. Kiwi is on his back with his eyes open, listening to the Maze the way other people listen to weather.

Duccarian sits near the hut line with Frodge tucked against his side, the duck breathing slow and warm in the dark. He is not watching anything specific. He is just present, the way philosophers are present, like bearing witness to a thing is its own kind of work.

SomeDucks sleeps lightly nearby, one hand resting flat on the ground, fingers loose.

From inside the new hut, Galaxy's breathing is audible, deep and steady, the rhythm of something that burns more fuel than a human body and needs more dark to recover.

And Rabbit is awake.

He has been awake since the light cooled.

He lies on his back in the sleeping area and listens to Aurora's Rest at night for the first time and tries to separate the sounds into categories that make sense. The waterfall, constant, mechanical underneath the natural surface of it. The air moving through Maze corridors, breathing out through the door gaps like the structure exhales. And then the screams.

He heard his first one an hour into the dark and his whole body went rigid before his mind did. He had reached for something to hold and found only ground.

Ninnginni, lying three feet away, had said without moving, "Quigg. Mid-corridor. Far side of the western wall. Not close."

Rabbit had stared at the ceiling. "How do you know."

"The echo bounces twice before it reaches us. Close ones bounce once. Direct ones do not bounce at all." A pause. "You learn the difference."

Rabbit said nothing.

He is still learning the difference now.

The unusual sound begins deep into the night, when most of the camp has finally settled.

Rabbit hears it first because he is the only one still fully awake and because it is nothing like anything he has catalogued so far. Where the Quigg screams are sharp and alive and built from biology and metal in equal measure, this sound is purely mechanical. Slow. Deliberate. Vertical. Metal against metal grinding in a patient downward register, like something enormous being asked to move against its own weight.

He sits up.

Kiwi sits up at the same moment, across the sleeping area, eyes already scanning.

Then Ut. Then janhk., who was never fully asleep. Lysrix is already standing.

Rally mally opens his eyes and does not ask what the sound is. He listens to it for three seconds and then stands without a word.

The sound is coming from the center of Aurora's Rest.

From the ground.

Rabbit is upright now, turning toward the platform in the middle of the clearing. It is moving. Slowly, grinding upward from below with more noise than it makes during the day, like the mechanism was not built for night use and is straining against the hour. The platform rises in increments, shuddering once, then continuing.

The Quigg screams in the Maze have gone quiet.

That detail lands on the camp like a stone into still water.

Duccarian is awake now, Frodge pressed against his chest, the duck alert and restless, head turning toward the elevator platform and back. SomeDucks sits up and watches without speaking. Galaxy steps out of the hut doorway and fills the frame of it, watching from there.

Rally mally moves to the center of camp, positions himself between the group and the platform, and raises one hand. Nobody approaches. Nobody speaks above a murmur.

Rabbit looks at Ninnginni. "Is this normal."

Ninnginni watches the rising platform. "The elevator comes during the day. Not at night." A pause. "This is not normal."

They wait.

The platform grinds upward.

Rabbit watches the process and understands something about Aurora's Rest that no one explained to him directly. The elevator is the only thing connecting this place to whatever is below. It has risen every day with new Peeps, new supplies, new instructions. It is the mouth of the system. And now the system is speaking in the middle of the night, which means the system does not keep regular hours. Which means it is not just running. It is watching. And it is choosing its moments.

The platform reaches the surface with a final grinding shudder and locks.

The sound dies.

The Quigg screams do not resume.

The whole structure holds its breath.

On the platform, slumped against the far rail, is a person. Unconscious. And beside them, held flat under one hand as though placed there with intention, a folded note.

space fan is moving before Rally mally finishes his scan. She crosses the distance fast and drops beside the figure, hands already checking airway, pulse, skull, neck, running the sequence with the efficiency of someone who has done field medicine in the dark before.

Lysrix is beside her in seconds.

Rabbit watches from the group, close enough to see, far enough not to crowd. He watches space fan work and understands that this camp has already learned what emergency looks like and has assigned roles to it without needing to discuss them.

Lysrix looks up at Rally mally. "Alive. Unconscious. Breathing normally. No visible wounds. Possible sedation."

Rally mally exhales once. "Get him inside."

Galaxy steps forward from the hut doorway without being asked. He crouches beside the unconscious figure and lifts them with the careful deliberateness of something that knows exactly how much strength it is choosing not to use. He carries them toward the medic hut like the weight is nothing and like the gentleness is everything.

The camp watches this and says nothing.

Duccarian holds Frodge and the duck has gone still, watching Galaxy move with that particular attention that looks like it understands more than it should.

Rally mally picks up the note from the platform. He unfolds it. He reads it once to himself and his expression does not change in the way that means it has changed completely, just below the surface.

He hands it to Duccarian.

Duccarian reads it aloud. His voice is steady and even, the voice of someone who knows that how a message lands changes what people do with it.

Hello Peeps.
Tomorrow is a big day for you.
You will see beacons of light.
Three to be exact.
Rising from the Maze.
They are supply drops that you WILL have to retrieve by Day 8.
And in case I don't see ya.
Good morning.
Good evening.
And good night.

P.S. The supply drops are guarded. You might want to bring some friends.

Duccarian folds the note along its original crease and holds it.

The camp is quiet for a moment.

Then everyone starts thinking at once.

Kiwi speaks first, low and focused. "Three beacons. Separate locations. That means three separate runs or one team splitting inside."

Ut already has the map cloth in his hands. "We do not know the current corridor configuration. The Maze shifted last night. The routes from yesterday do not apply."

janhk. says, "Guarded. The note says guarded. That is a warning dressed as a postscript."

Nightmare says, "Good." Simply. Without performance. Like he has been waiting for something that requires more than endurance.

Rabbit looks at the note in Duccarian's hands and then at the Maze doors and then at the people around him. He arrived this afternoon. He has been in Aurora's Rest for less than one full rotation of the light. He watched a burial. He is now being told tomorrow he will probably go into the Maze. He says, to no one in particular, "It escalates fast."

Ninnginni answers without irony. "Yes."

SomeDucks watches the note. "The sign-off is familiar. Comfortable. It wants us to feel like we have a relationship with it."

Duccarian says, "We do. We just did not choose it."

The name that comes next nobody says aloud immediately. It arrives the way it always does in Aurora's Rest, sideways, carried by implication rather than declaration.

SomeDucks says it quietly. "Schwalbe."

He does not explain it. He does not need to. The camp knows the name from a past note, the same careful handwriting, the same tone that sits just at the edge of warmth without ever crossing into it. They do not know a face. They do not know a position. They only know the name and the fact that whoever writes it can control when the elevator moves.

Rally mally does not confirm or deny. He looks at the note and then at the Maze doors and then at his people.

"Three beacons," he says. "Inside the Maze. Guarded. We do not know by what or how many." He pauses. "That is tomorrow's problem. Tonight's problem is rest."

Ut says, "I need an hour before sleep to start the route plan—"

"Rest first," Rally mally says. Not harsh. Final.

Ut closes the map cloth.

space fan and Lysrix work quietly in the medic hut. The unconscious Peep is laid on the flattest surface available. space fan checks the pulse again, checks the pupils, checks for bruising from the elevator mechanism. Minor pressure marks on the shoulders and one knee, consistent with a body sliding against the platform railing during transit. Nothing structural. Nothing internal that she can find.

Lysrix says quietly, "Sedated."

space fan nods. "Something in the elevator. Maybe the drop itself. He will wake." She checks the breathing once more. "Probably by morning."

They make him comfortable with what they have. A folded cloth under his head. A cover across his chest. space fan stays in the medic hut entrance for a while watching the breathing rise and fall before Lysrix puts a hand on her shoulder and steers her back.

"You cannot watch him into consciousness," Lysrix says.

space fan says, "I know."

She goes back anyway. Lysrix lets her.

The camp settles in pieces. Ninnginni goes back down quickly, running on empty. Toad sits up for another twenty minutes cataloguing tomorrow's supply implications in his head before finally lying flat. Cyrus falls asleep mid-thought about route reinforcement.

Kiwi lies on his back with the map of the Maze building itself behind his eyes. He cannot stop building it. He does not try. He lets the corridors arrange themselves and notes the gaps where the knowledge ends.

Rabbit does not sleep.

He lies still and listens and builds a different kind of map. Not corridors but people. Rally mally, who leads by being unmoveable. Lysrix, who watches everything and says the exact minimum. janhk., who reads the ground like a language. Nightmare, who was pleased about the note. Galaxy, who carried an unconscious stranger with the care of something that remembers tenderness even after the body that holds it has been changed completely.

He thinks about the burial this afternoon.

He did not know her name until after she was in the ground.

He does not plan to let that happen again.

Near the end of the night Duccarian sits with his back against the shelter wall and Frodge asleep in the warmth between his arm and his side. SomeDucks is nearby, not quite asleep, not quite awake.

Duccarian speaks quietly into the dark.

"The Maze gives things wrapped in cost. The water came with Doomfox. Whatever those beacons are holding will come with whatever is guarding them." He pauses. "It is not cruelty. It is a system. A system does not hate. It is indifferent. And indifference at this scale looks exactly like cruelty from the inside."

SomeDucks says, almost asleep, "Is there a difference."

Duccarian considers this. "For the Maze, no. For us, yes. Because if it is cruelty we are victims. If it is a system we are participants. And participants can learn the rules."

Frodge shifts once and settles deeper.

Duccarian presses his hand over the duck's back and does not say anything else.

Galaxy stands in the doorway of his hut and watches the Maze doors. He does not sleep again after the elevator. He watches from there, still and patient, the way something that has been inside the corridors watches walls. He knows what is behind them better than any of the others. He remembers the corridors from the inside, from when he was still something the Maze could direct instead of something it had to account for.

He does not think about C A R L directly.

But he watches the doors the way you watch something when you know what is standing on the other side of it.

He stays there until the first fraction of warmth touches the artificial sky. Then he goes back inside and lies down and closes his eyes.

Not sleeping.

Just waiting for morning.

The waterfall runs.

The Quigg screams resume eventually, far away and disorganized, like patrols without direction. The Maze shifts once in the early hours, a single long grind that settles back into silence. The orchard stones above pinkfml's grave do not move.

In the medic hut, JIKOSU breathes steadily, somewhere between whatever the elevator did to him and whatever Aurora's Rest will ask of him when his eyes open.

The note is folded in Duccarian's hand.

Three beacons. Day 8. Guarded.

The Maze has stopped waiting for them to find it.

Now it is asking them in.

Night 7 ends quietly.

But quiet in Aurora's Rest has started to mean something different.

It used to mean safe.

Now it means the Maze is thinking.

And the distance between those two things is exactly the size of a folded note left on an elevator platform in the dark.

That is the part that should worry them most.
