[[DAY]]
DAY 7 — THE VILLAGE AND THE WATER
[[NARRATIVE]]
Day 7 arrives like it does not know what happened.
The waterfall sound fills Aurora's Rest before anyone opens their eyes. It is constant and smooth and strangely perfect, a sheet of water falling into a dark basin that should overflow but never does. The Peeps do not know it recirculates. To them it looks like the world has a hole in it and something kind decided to pour forever into the gap.
It is the first morning that does not start in silence.
Rally mally is already standing.
He calls headcount with the same voice he always uses, flat and present, like certainty is a posture he chose a long time ago and has not put down since.
Kiwi answers and sits up straight like he was not asleep.
Lysrix answers and marks who looks functional.
Ut answers and pulls out his map cloth before Rally finishes saying his name.
janhk. answers and checks the stone seam near his sleeping spot the way some people check a pulse.
Ninnginni answers and manages half a yawn before catching himself.
space fan answers while counting the medical supply strips by touch.
-MAD$- answers while moving containers, already working.
Duccarian answers quietly, sitting near the waterfall zone edge, eyes calm.
Nightmare answers without looking at Rally, staring at the Maze doors like they owe him something.
Cyrus answers and rubs the back of his neck, working out a knot.
SomeDucks answers slowly, watching the waterfall from a distance, listening to a sound only he seems to be cataloguing.
Toad answers and immediately checks the fermentation pot, lifting the cloth seal an inch and inhaling with professional focus.
Popcorn Rya answers and pulls her knees up and looks at the space where Doomfox used to sit when the runner team came back.
pinkfml answers in a whisper. She is already awake, already sitting, already staring at the Maze doors. She answers her own name like she is confirming something she is not sure of.
Rally mally pauses after her name for a half second.
Then he says the last name.
"GalaxyTwentea."
From the cage, a claw taps the bar once. Then a forearm drops to the dirt and scratches a single mark. Present.
Rally mally looks at the mark, then looks at the cage, then looks at the group.
He speaks before anyone can start the day's work.
"I want to say something about Galaxy."
The camp pays attention the way tired people do, with their bodies still and their eyes awake.
Rally mally speaks measured and direct. "He has worked. He has listened. He has not hurt anyone. He warned us in Night 6 before some of us were even watching." He pauses. "We built that cage because we were afraid. I am still afraid. But I think we are keeping him locked because it is easy, not because it is right."
Ut says immediately, "Rules first."
space fan says, "He should be free."
Kiwi says, "Daytime only. He earns the nights."
janhk. says, "If he bolts toward the Maze we cannot stop him."
Nightmare says, "He is not a person."
SomeDucks says quietly, "He remembers being one."
Rally mally lets the responses land, then speaks. "Galaxy is free during daylight hours. He sleeps in a structure we build for him, not the cage. He stays inside Aurora's Rest boundaries. He does not approach the Maze doors without a runner team present."
He looks at the cage. "Those are the rules. If he breaks them we reassess."
He walks to the cage latch himself.
He opens it.
Galaxy does not burst out.
He steps out slowly, unfolding to full height, blinking in the open light. He is enormous and wrong shaped and somehow careful, the way something strong can choose to take up less space than it owns. He stands in the morning air and turns his head once around the full perimeter of Aurora's Rest like he is measuring it with something other than eyes.
Then he scratches in the dirt.
THANK YOU.
space fan exhales like she has been holding it since Night 2.
Toad says, "Well. Good morning then."
Ninnginni grins despite himself. "He has better manners than Nightmare."
Nightmare says, "I am not competing with a lizard."
Kiwi watches Galaxy for a long moment, then looks away, something uncoiling in his shoulders.

The morning builds into the busiest day Aurora's Rest has seen.
Rally mally organizes work into three streams running at once.
Ut and janhk. plan the water transport system, sketching shallow channel lines and testing container weight from the waterfall basin to the central camp. They argue quietly about gradient and stone composition like engineers who forgot they were trapped.
Cyrus and -MAD−worktheorchardfarmland,turningsoilrowsandplantingseedsfromthesupplycrates.−MAD- work the orchard farmland, turning soil rows and planting seeds from the supply crates. -MAD
−worktheorchardfarmland,turningsoilrowsandplantingseedsfromthesupplycrates.−MAD- counts seeds like currency. Cyrus talks to himself about root depth and sun angles, referencing a sun that is not real but behaves like one.

Toad tends the fermentation pot with the patience of a person who has decided that patience is the skill Aurora's Rest cannot teach but desperately needs. He explains the process to anyone within earshot.
"We need four more days. Maybe three if the warmth holds." He checks the cloth seal. "The sugar from the orchard scraps is converting. When it is done it will not taste like anything good. It will taste like we planned to be here long enough to make it."
Ninnginni leans over his shoulder. "What does that taste like."
Toad replaces the seal carefully. "Like a reason."

And then there is Galaxy building his own hut.
Rally mally assigns Cyrus to design it and Galaxy to build it and the combination is immediately, unexpectedly, something worth watching.
Cyrus sketches a low structure in the dirt, slightly away from the main sleeping area, sturdier than the others, wider at the base, with a roof line that slopes against the direction the Maze sounds come from. He explains the reasoning to Galaxy with hand gestures and drawn lines.
Galaxy watches, then picks up a log that would take three Peeps to move and carries it to the marked spot with the particular casualness of something that has recently discovered it is very strong.
Cyrus stares.
Galaxy scratches in the dirt.
WHERE.
Cyrus points. Galaxy places the log precisely. He does not drop it. He sets it down.
"He is better at this than half the crew," Cyrus mutters.
janhk. walks past carrying rope. "Do not let Nightmare hear you say that."
By midmorning the frame is up. By midday the walls are taking shape. Galaxy works with methodical focus, and when he pauses to scratch something in the dirt it is always practical. CORNER WEAK. THIS SIDE. MORE HERE. The hut becomes real faster than anything they have built, because the builder does not get tired in the normal way, and the builder does not second-guess the weight of things.
When the walls are standing Galaxy steps inside it, turns around once, and lies down to test the space. He fits, barely, but intentionally. He has built it to his exact dimensions.
Then he scratches from inside it.
GOOD.
Popcorn Rya, watching from the treeline, says, "He designed a house for himself. He just designed a house."
SomeDucks says, "He remembers what home means."
Duccarian, sitting nearby with his back against a tree, says nothing, but watches Galaxy's face through the hut doorway with an expression that is working something through.

The Duccarian and Frodge moment comes quietly, the way important things do.
SomeDucks had been watching the duck since morning, observing the way it moved through the waterfall zone without fear, stepping between wet stones like the moss and the mist were built for small things. He had not tried to touch it. He watched it the way he watched everything, cataloguing, letting patterns arrange themselves.
When Duccarian comes to sit by the waterfall basin, SomeDucks simply does not move away.
Frodge notices Duccarian first.
The duck walks toward him with that particular duck logic that ignores all normal social reasoning, steps directly up to his boot, and looks up at him.
Duccarian looks down.
He does not reach for the duck. He just waits.
Frodge climbs onto his knee.
SomeDucks says, softly surprised, "He does not do that."
Duccarian looks at the duck sitting on his knee, feathers damp from the basin mist, small eyes steady and unafraid.
He says, "This is the only living thing in Aurora's Rest that does not know it should be terrified."
SomeDucks thinks about that. "Maybe that is what we need. One thing that does not know."
Duccarian lifts Frodge gently with both hands, turning the duck to look at him properly. Frodge endures this without complaint.
"The Maze made all of this," Duccarian says, still looking at the duck. "The walls. The creatures. The traps. The gas. The lever that punished Doomfox for doing exactly what it asked him to do." He pauses. "And then it put a duck in the waterfall."
SomeDucks watches him. "You think that was a mistake."
Duccarian shakes his head. "I think the Maze planned every single thing except this. The duck found a crack in the design. Something small enough to slip through unnoticed."
Frodge makes a small sound.
Duccarian says, "Exactly."
SomeDucks almost laughs. Not quite. But almost.
From the camp, Ninnginni spots Duccarian sitting with a duck on his knee speaking to it philosophically while the waterfall falls behind him, and he nudges Toad and points.
Toad watches for a moment. "Is he having a conversation with the duck."
Ninnginni whispers, "I think the duck is winning."
Toad watches another moment. "Good."
Popcorn Rya comes over when she hears them, sees the scene, and laughs. A real one, sudden and slightly shocked at itself, the kind of laugh that happens before your body remembers it is not supposed to.
Rally mally hears it from across the camp and does not smile exactly, but something in his jaw unclenches a degree.
pinkfml hears it too, from where she is sitting against the orchard fence line.
She looks up.
She watches Popcorn Rya laughing.
She watches Duccarian holding the duck.
She almost smiles.
Then she looks back at the Maze doors and the almost-smile dissolves like it was borrowed.

[[NARRATIVE]]
pinkfml's day has a different texture from everyone else's.
It is not dramatic. It does not announce itself.
It is the texture of something quietly coming loose.
She does her morning tasks, carrying water, stacking supply cloth, sitting where she is supposed to sit. But she keeps losing the thread of each task halfway through. She sets a container down and forgets she was carrying it. She stands in the middle of the orchard path for a few seconds too long before remembering which direction she was going.
space fan notices. She does not say anything direct. She just starts positioning herself closer to pinkfml's orbit, appearing near her at regular intervals, handing her things to do that are simple and require full hands.
Lysrix notices and says nothing at all. She watches from a distance with eyes that are counting something.
At one point pinkfml asks space fan a question in a flat, careful voice.
"Did he scream for long."
space fan does not pretend not to understand. "pinkfml—"
"Doomfox." Still flat. Still careful. "When the Quigg took him. Did it last long."
space fan holds her gaze. "No."
pinkfml nods once, like confirming data. She goes back to stacking supply cloth.
Later, near the orchard fence, pinkfml looks at her hands for a long time. They are clean. She has washed them. But she turns them over and over like there is something on them that normal washing cannot reach.
Duccarian passes and sees this.
He does not stop. But he looks at her hands, and then at her face, and something shifts in his expression that is not quite pity and not quite recognition but is somewhere between them.
He does not say anything.
He is not sure yet that words would reach where she is.

In the mid afternoon, pinkfml goes to the waterfall zone alone.
She tells no one. There is no dramatic departure. She simply is in camp, and then she is not.
The waterfall sound covers the absence.
She stands at the basin edge, looking at the water fall. She has been here before. The familiarity of the sound is why she comes. It is the only thing in Aurora's Rest that does not change its mind, that does not get taken, that does not stop in the night.
The stones near the basin edge are wet. They are always wet. The mist from the fall keeps them damp and green with a thin layer of moss that looks like it belongs in a world without walls.
pinkfml crouches near the edge and puts her hands in the water.
She is washing them again.
She watches the water move over her fingers and tries to feel something that is not the shape of Doomfox reaching toward her through the closing corridor.
She does not cry.
She has not cried since Night 4.
She shifts her weight on the wet stone to stand up.
Her foot slips.
It is not a movie slip. There is no suspended moment, no chance to catch it. One foot goes sideways on the moss and she drops fast, hip catching the basin rim, head striking the stone ledge below the fall with a sound that is too quiet for what it means.
She slides into the shallow edge of the basin and does not move.
The waterfall continues.
Smooth. Endless. Indifferent.

Frodge finds her first.
The duck had been moving through the waterfall zone on its own logic, stepping between stones, when it reaches the basin edge and stops. It stands at the rim making a sound that is not its usual sound, a short repeated noise with urgency built into it that ducks should not know how to carry.
Duccarian hears it.
He is not far. He had come back to the waterfall zone after the afternoon work period, Frodge's usual territory, and when he hears the sound he moves without deciding to.
He sees her in the basin shallows.
He shouts.
The sound that comes out of him is not a word. It is just volume, the specific volume of someone whose body understands before their mind does.
space fan reaches the waterfall zone at a run. Lysrix behind her. Rally mally from the treeline, moving fast with his jaw already set.
They get pinkfml out of the water.
space fan works fast and certain and does not speak while she works. She checks breathing. She checks pulse. She checks the wound at the temple where the stone caught her and the damage it did in that fraction of a second on wet rock.
She sits back.
She shakes her head once.
The waterfall continues.

[[NARRATIVE]]
The camp does not process it immediately.
Rally mally stands and looks at pinkfml's face and does not speak for a long time. When he finally turns, his expression is not grief yet. It is the face of a man holding something in both hands that he does not know how to put down.
space fan stays kneeling beside her, hands still, not moving away.
Kiwi walks to the orchard fence line and grips the wood and stares at nothing and does not turn around for a while.
Ut sits down where he is standing and pulls out his map cloth and stares at it without marking anything.
janhk. goes to the basin edge and examines the stone. He studies the moss, the slope, the angle of the ledge. He does it with the same focused attention he gives to pressure plates and seam lines. He is looking for the mechanism. He cannot find one. That is somehow the worst part.
Ninnginni sits against a tree and puts his face in his hands.
-MAD$- sets down the supply container he was carrying very carefully and does not pick it up again.
Cyrus just says, "No," quietly, to no one, once.
Toad looks at his fermentation pot.
He looks at it for a long time.
SomeDucks stands near the waterfall and watches the water fall and says nothing for a while. Then he says, almost to himself, "She was trying to clean her hands."
Nightmare stands apart from the group and looks at pinkfml's stillness. He says, in his flat honest way, "The Maze did not even need to touch her." He does not say it cruelly. He says it like a fact he wishes was wrong. "It just needed to take enough from her that she stopped being careful."
Nobody answers him.
Because nobody can argue with it.
GalaxyTwentea(dino enthusiast) approaches slowly. The others make room without being asked. Galaxy stands over pinkfml for a moment, head low, and the mechanical quality of her raptor body seems to soften somehow, or maybe the group's perception of it does.
She crouches and scratches in the dirt beside pinkfml's hand.
SORRY.
Popcorn Rya makes a sound.
She had been holding herself together since they found her, keeping the weight back through pure posture, but Galaxy's single scratched word breaks something in the wall of it and she cries the way exhausted people cry, without elegance, leaning against the tree behind her and not trying to stop.
Duccarian holds Frodge and does not speak for a long time.
When he does, he speaks the way he sometimes does, like the thought has been waiting and the silence finally gave it room.
"Aurora's Rest is becoming a village," he says. "The hut. The water channels. The orchard rows. The beer fermenting in the pot." He pauses. "Villages bury people." He looks at pinkfml's face. "That is not a failure of the village. That is what villages are. Places where lives happen. Including the ends of them."
SomeDucks says, soft and precise, "The Maze built a cage. And we built a home inside it. It did not plan for that."
Duccarian nods. "So it reminded us of the walls."

They bury pinkfml in the orchard.
Not deep in it. Near the edge, where the grass meets the first row of planted seeds, where the soil is soft and the waterfall sound reaches. Rally mally decides this. He does not ask for opinions and nobody offers them.
They use their hands and the flat edge of a digging tool. The work is quiet. Galaxy carries the stones they use as markers, choosing them from the waterfall zone with what looks like deliberate attention to size and shape. Nobody tells him to do this. He just does it.
Duccarian holds Frodge through all of it.
Frodge does not struggle. Does not make noise. As if it understands, or as if proximity to Duccarian is enough to make strange things feel less strange.
When it is done Rally mally stands at the head of the burial plot and opens his mouth and then closes it again.
Lysrix speaks instead, simply, without ceremony. "She was here. She tried. The Maze was harder than that was enough."
Space fan keeps her eyes on the orchard rows.
Kiwi crouches and touches the soil once, then stands.
Toad is the last to move away from the grave.
He walks back to the fermentation pot. He lifts the cloth seal. He checks the progress.
He replaces the seal.
He says nothing out loud.
But he marks something in the dirt beside the pot, a small scratched line, a private notation.
Popcorn Rya sees it and asks, voice still rough, "What is that."
Toad answers, "A note to myself."
She waits.
He exhales. "The beer will finish in three days. I just wanted to remember who it was supposed to be for."
Popcorn Rya looks at the pot.
She does not say anything.
She sits down beside it, close enough to hear if it makes a sound.

The light begins to cool.
The Maze doors start their grinding cycle as though nothing happened.
Rally mally calls everyone in.
They come. All of them.
The count is smaller than it was on Day 1 and they all feel the arithmetic of it in different ways.
As the camp settles into night formation, Duccarian finds a place near the shelter line with Frodge still against his chest. The duck shifts once, settling deeper into the warmth of him, and Duccarian presses his hand over its small back.
The waterfall falls in the dark.
The Maze shifts.
The Quigg screams begin their night circuit far away in the stone corridors.
And Aurora's Rest holds its shape. Water in the basin. Seeds in the soil. Beer breathing in the pot. A hut built by something that used to be human. A grave in the orchard with waterfall stones at its head.
A village.
A cage.
Both at once.
The Maze does not care which word they use.

Day 7 ends with the waterfall sounding like it always has.
The Maze does not celebrate.
It does not gloat.
It simply continues.
And somewhere in the corridors, the walls grind into a new configuration, and something patient and enormous adjusts its understanding of the place, and the people in it, and how much more it has to teach them.
